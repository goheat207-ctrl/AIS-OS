01Floor 01 · Foundation
The brain · knows

Context

It knows your business.

Wiki, decisions log, intake answers, who-works-here, what-you-sell, what-you-stand-for. Without it, every prompt starts cold and every output sounds like nobody. Most setups die on this floor — they confuse data with interpretation. The wiki is not a doc dump. The decisions log is not a Slack archive.

▸ Stands on: nothing. This is the ground.
Binary "in place" test · 01 DRY-RUN
"What does this business do, and who works here?"
A fresh Claude session answers cold — no browsing, no paste.
Knowledge base
02Floor 02 · Wiring
The wiring · reaches

Connections

It reaches your stuff.

MCPs, APIs, integrations — calendar, tasks, mail, drive, the stack you actually live in. Once Context is in place, Connections stop being a convenience and start being structural. The OS pulls live data instead of begging you for paste.

▲ Stands on: Context. Without it, connections retrieve noise.
Binary "in place" test · 02 LIVE-DATA
"What's on my calendar tomorrow, and what tasks are due?"
Returns live data. No paste. No "could you share…?"
MCP / API graph
03Floor 03 · Workshop
The hands · does

Capabilities

It knows how to do the work.

Skills and sub-agents that turn a short phrase into a multi-step workflow producing an actual artifact. Lego principle: smallest steps first, deterministic before agentic, validated before chained. Boring is beautiful — and workflows beat agents until they don't.

▲▲ Stands on: Context + Connections. Skills without either are demos.
Binary "in place" test · 03 ARTIFACT
A short phrase triggers a multi-step workflow.
Out the other end: a real artifact. A doc, a draft, a brief, a file.
Skill chain · validated
04Floor 04 · The OS is alive
The pulse · runs

Cadence

It runs without being asked.

Scheduled standups, ambient nudges, hooks, cron, launchd. The OS works while you sleep. Laptop closed, a brief lands in your inbox. A teammate messages your AIOS at 11pm and gets a real answer in your voice. This is the line between an AIOS and a pile of automations.

▲▲▲ Stands on: all three below. Skip any and Cadence runs into the dark.
Binary "in place" test · 04 RUNNING
Laptop closed. Something happens.
A brief lands in your inbox. The reply goes out. The OS doesn't wait.

The litmus moment
