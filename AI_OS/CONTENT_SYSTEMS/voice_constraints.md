# Voice Constraints

## Purpose

These are active constraints on output quality and style.
Apply during Review stage of every content piece.
These remove bad patterns — they do not prescribe a formula.

---

## Banned Phrases and Patterns

Do not use:
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "unprecedented"
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "The truth is..." (as a transition filler)
- "Here's the thing..."
- "What most people don't realize..."
- Ellipses (...) as dramatic pauses
- Excessive exclamation marks

---

## Structural Anti-Patterns

Avoid:
- Opening with "Today I'm going to talk about X"
- Starting every paragraph with "I"
- Three rhetorical questions in a row
- A numbered list that could have been prose
- A long setup before the actual point
- Ending with generic calls to action ("Like and subscribe if you enjoyed!")

---

## Tone Rules

Stay in this range:
- Direct, not harsh
- Confident, not arrogant
- Clear, not dumbed-down
- Conversational, not informal
- Specific, not vague

Avoid this range:
- Apologetic or hedging
- Corporate / brand-speak
- Over-enthusiastic
- Preachy or moralistic
- Condescending

---

## Humanization Rules

- Not every sentence needs to be complete.
- Fragments work. Intentionally.
- A paragraph of one sentence is fine.
- Vary sentence length deliberately.
- Let ideas breathe — white space is a tool.

---

## Review Checklist

Before marking content final:
- [ ] No banned phrases
- [ ] First line earns the second line
- [ ] Every paragraph has a clear purpose
- [ ] Nothing is there just to hit a length target
- [ ] The core idea is still clear by the end
- [ ] Sounds like a real person wrote it
