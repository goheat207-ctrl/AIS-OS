# Content Pillars

## Purpose

Pillars define the thematic territory of the content.
They provide direction without scripting.
They answer: "what topics are in scope?"

---

## How to Use This File

This file is a template. Fill it in for each content project.
Copy the structure to your project CLAUDE.md under "Content Pillars."

Pillars should be:
- Specific to your domain and audience
- Broad enough to generate many ideas
- Distinct enough that each pillar doesn't overlap heavily

## Example: Trading Content Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Psychology and Behavior
**Theme:** The mental side of trading — why people lose when they know better.
**Why it matters:** Most traders have the knowledge. The gap is behavior.
**Examples:** revenge trading, overconfidence after wins, cutting winners early

### Pillar 2 — Systems and Structure
**Theme:** How professional traders structure their process, day, and decisions.
**Why it matters:** Amateurs react. Professionals operate from systems.
**Examples:** pre-market routine, risk rules, journaling process

### Pillar 3 — Setup Breakdowns
**Theme:** Specific technical patterns and how to read them.
**Why it matters:** Pattern recognition is learnable with the right examples.
**Examples:** scanner hits, live trade breakdowns, A-setup anatomy

## Pillar Discipline

Pillars should guide direction — not lock every piece into a category.
If a piece doesn't fit a pillar but is clearly worth making: make it.

Pillars are a creative organizing tool. Not a content cage.
