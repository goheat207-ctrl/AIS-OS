# Research Workflow

## Purpose

This file defines how research is collected, processed, and synthesized.
It applies to all research-domain projects.

---

## Research Workflow Stages

```
Question → Intake → Processing → Synthesis → Output → Archive
```

---

## Stage 1 — Question

Before collecting any sources: define the research question precisely.

A good research question:
- Is specific enough that you'd know when it's answered
- Has a defined scope (what's in, what's out)
- Has a clear output format (memo, briefing, database, analysis)

A bad research question: "learn about X" — no defined end state.

---

## Stage 2 — Intake

Collect sources relevant to the question.
For each source captured:

```
Source: [title and URL or reference]
Credibility: [primary source / secondary / opinion / unknown]
Key claims:
  - [claim 1]
  - [claim 2]
Conflicts with: [any other sources that contradict this?]
```

Store each source in the project `sources\` folder.
Do not synthesize during intake — capture first, analyze second.

---

## Stage 3 — Processing

Read and annotate all sources.
Per source:
- What is the core argument or finding?
- What evidence supports it?
- What is the credibility of the source?
- How does it relate to the other sources?

Store processing notes in project `notes\` folder.

---

## Stage 4 — Synthesis

Cross-source analysis:
- What do the sources agree on? (higher confidence)
- Where do they conflict? (investigate or flag uncertainty)
- What gaps remain? (what questions are still unanswered?)
- What is the most defensible conclusion given the evidence?

Structure synthesis as:
```
Conclusion: [answer to the research question]
Confidence: [high / medium / low] because [why]
Key supporting evidence:
  - [point 1]
  - [point 2]
Conflicts / uncertainties:
  - [what remains unclear]
```

---

## Stage 5 — Output

Format for the intended audience.
Apply the principle: **conclusion first, evidence second, sources last.**

Never bury the finding at the bottom.
The reader should know the answer in the first paragraph.

---

## Stage 6 — Archive

Store final output in the correct project location.
Update session_handoff.md if the research session is part of a larger project.

---

## AI Role in Research

AI can:
- Summarize individual sources
- Identify patterns and conflicts across sources
- Draft synthesis documents
- Format final outputs
- Generate follow-up questions

AI cannot:
- Verify facts from real-time sources
- Assess the credibility of sources it hasn't read
- Replace domain expertise in evaluating evidence quality
