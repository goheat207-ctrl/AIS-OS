## Core Thesis

> Struggling traders don't have bad strategies. They have execution errors.

SMB ran an experiment: 20 stuck traders. Fixed 5 mistakes (not strategies). 16 of 20 back on track in 60 days.

---

## The 5 Mistakes

### Mistake 1 — Holding runners past 10:30 AM

**Data:** 5,000 momentum trades analyzed:
- 73% of >5% first-hour runners made their HOD before 10:30
- 89% made HOD before 11:00
- Only 11% made new highs after 11:00

**Why it happens:** Momentum urgency (FOMO, institutional size execution) fades after 10:30. Algos and options market makers step in and chop the move.

**Fix:** If you enter a momentum trade between 9:30-10:00, set an alarm for 10:25. Scale out or exit entirely. No exceptions for 30 days.

**Real result:** One trader was giving back $14,000/year in profit fade by holding past 10:30.

---

### Mistake 2 — Adding to losing positions (3 forms)

All three are the same mistake — adding risk when expected value is NOT improving:

1. **Averaging down** — buying more after price drops, "bluffing your way out of a bad situation"
2. **Adding without EV expanding** — stock flatlines or doesn't confirm, you add anyway hoping it will work
3. **Re-entering the same bad trade** — stopped out, back in 3 minutes. "Same idea, same lack of confirmation, just more hope."

> "Price alone has zero relevance to the expected value of a trade."

**Fix:** You are only allowed to add when the trade is already working AND confirming your thesis. Before every add: "What new information improved this trade? Would I take this fresh right here?"

> "Elite traders don't add because they want to be right. They add because the market proved them right."

---

### Mistake 3 — Trading without tape confirmation

**Data:** Two groups trading identical setups:
- Chart-only group: 54% win rate, 1.6 R:R
- Tape-confirmation group: 68% win rate, higher R:R, significantly more monthly profit

**4 tape confirmation signals:**
1. Size on offer thinning (big seller getting cut through)
2. Sweep orders (multiple price levels cleared at once — institutional buying)
3. Bids stacking on pullbacks (supportive buying, continuation signal)
4. Print speed accelerating (urgency increasing)

**Rule:** Require 3 of 4 signals to confirm entry. 1 or 0 signals = wait or don't size up.

> "The chart shows where price has been. The tape shows who is in control right now."

---

### Mistake 4 — Ignoring 3 red bars after a strong move

After 5+ consecutive green bars, 3 consecutive red bars = momentum shift signal.

**Rule:**
1. Draw a line at the high of the third red bar
2. If next bar takes out that high immediately → can hold
3. If it does NOT → reduce size or exit. 60%+ of remaining profits will be given back if you hold.

> "If you see those three red bars and don't make a decision, that's not trading. That's hoping."

---

### Mistake 5 — Not taking profits at resistance

**Data:** 5,000 intraday trades approaching identified resistance:
- First test of resistance: 68% chance it holds (fails to break)
- Second test: 54% hold rate
- Third test: 42% hold rate

Most traders hold through resistance hoping for the 32% breakout outcome. When it rejects, they give it all back.

**Fix:** When stock approaches clear resistance (prior high, whole number, VWAP):
- Exit 50% of position 10 cents before the level
- If it breaks through: still have 50%, can re-add
- If it rejects: profits locked in at optimal spot

> "That is trading probabilities. Not trading hope."

## Key Takeaways for Blaine

| Mistake | Direct application |
|---------|--------------------|
| Holding past 10:30 | Set 10:25 alarm on every first-hour momentum trade |
| Averaging down | Check: "Would I take this fresh right now?" If no, the add is illegal |
| No tape confirmation | Need 3/4 signals before entry — L2 and T&S review is required |
| Ignoring 3 red bars | This is a rule to encode in the backtester and journal |
| Ignoring resistance | Mark resistance before entry. Partial exit offer set in advance |
