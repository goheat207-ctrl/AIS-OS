Here’s the **professional-grade prompt structure** you should use when asking AI about trading indicators. 📊

Most people ask vague questions like:

> “What indicators are good for scalping?”

That produces generic garbage.

Instead, you want to force the AI to think through:

- **Timeframe**
    
- **Market regime**
    
- **Trading style**
    
- **Goal of the indicator**
    
- **Execution environment**
    
- **What problem you're trying to solve**
    

---

# 🔧 MASTER PROMPT FRAMEWORK

Use this structure:

```text
I trade [market type] using a [trading style] strategy on the [timeframe(s)].

My goal is to use indicators specifically for:
- [entries]
- [exits]
- [trend confirmation]
- [momentum]
- [mean reversion]
- [scalping]
- [risk management]
- [volume confirmation]
- [fakeout detection]
- [dip buying]
- [parabolic exhaustion]
etc.

I want indicators that work specifically in:
- [small caps / large caps / forex / crypto / futures]
- [high volatility / low volatility]
- [trend markets / chop]
- [premarket / regular hours / after hours]

Prioritize:
- leading indicators over lagging
- real-world professional usage
- low indicator redundancy
- fast execution usefulness
- high signal-to-noise ratio

For each indicator explain:
1. What it measures
2. Why it works on this timeframe
3. Best settings
4. Ideal market conditions
5. Failure conditions
6. Common retail misuse
7. How professionals actually use it
8. Best indicator combinations
9. Whether it is leading, lagging, or contextual
10. Whether it is useful for entries, exits, or both

Avoid generic beginner advice.
Focus on statistical edge and execution quality.
```

---

# 📈 EXAMPLE — SMALL CAP SCALPING

```text
I trade U.S. small-cap momentum stocks using 1-minute and 5-minute charts.

I need indicators specifically for:
- HOD breakouts
- VWAP reclaim setups
- momentum continuation
- exhaustion detection
- fake breakout avoidance

Focus on indicators that work best in:
- high RVOL environments
- low float stocks
- fast volatility expansion
- intraday momentum trading

Prioritize:
- execution precision
- low lag
- professional intraday usage
- avoiding indicator redundancy

For each indicator explain:
- ideal settings
- why it works on lower timeframes
- failure conditions
- best combinations
- whether it helps with entries or exits
- how market makers exploit traders using it incorrectly

Avoid beginner indicators unless they have real edge.
```

# 🧠 BEST WAY TO ASK ABOUT INDICATORS

Instead of asking:

❌ “What’s the best indicator?”

Ask:

✅ “What indicators provide the best informational edge for THIS specific environment and THIS specific execution style?”

That changes the AI from:

- generic educator ❌  
    to:
    
- execution analyst ✅
    

---

# 🔬 THE MOST IMPORTANT THING

Indicators are NOT:

- magical signals
    
- prediction tools
    
- standalone systems
    

They are:

> **context filters and information compression tools**

Professionals use indicators to:

- measure participation
    
- measure momentum
    
- measure volatility
    
- locate liquidity
    
- identify trend structure
    
- confirm thesis quality
    

Retail traders use indicators as:

- buy/sell buttons ❌
    

That distinction matters massively.
