# Reading the Tape

The tape (Level 2 + Time & Sales) shows who is in control of a stock right now. The chart shows where price has been. The tape tells you what the chart can't.

> "The chart just gets you in the game. The tape tells you if you should stay in the game."
> — SMB Capital

---

## Why It Matters

SMB Capital data comparing two groups trading identical setups:
- Chart-only entries: 54% win rate
- Tape-confirmation entries: 68% win rate

Same strategy. Same stocks. 14% win rate gap just from checking the tape before entry.

---

## 4 Tape Confirmation Signals (Need 3 of 4 to Enter)

| Signal | What it looks like | What it means |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Offer thinning** | Big size on the ask, then it shrinks | Seller being absorbed — buyers overcoming resistance |
| **Sweep orders** | Multiple price levels cleared in quick succession | Institutional or large buyer with urgency |
| **Bids stacking** | Large bid size appearing on pullbacks | Supportive buying, continuation likely |
| **Print speed accelerating** | T&S printing faster than before | Urgency increasing, buyers pressing |

**1 or 0 signals present → wait or reduce size. Don't force the entry.**

## Held Bid — Original Concept

A **held bid** = large size sitting on the bid that repeatedly bounces price.

Example: 1.2M shares on the bid at $120 in NVDA. Price keeps bouncing off that level without breaking down — buyers are confident and defending.

**Two outcomes to watch:**
- If price keeps bouncing off the bid → bullish, possible long entry with tight stop below bid
- If sellers start hitting that bid and size starts decrementing → potential momentum short once bid is fully taken out

Context check before acting on held bid:
- Is NASDAQ weak?
- Is SMH weak?
- Is broader market tape a tailwind or headwind?

## Tape vs. Chart

| The chart tells you | The tape tells you |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| Where price has been | Who is in control right now |
| Whether a setup exists | Whether to take the setup |
| Structure and levels | Actual order flow at those levels |
| A bull flag formed | If there's a real buyer at the breakout |

---

## Tape at Resistance

A chart breakout means nothing if the tape is screaming against it.

Before entering a breakout:
- Is the offer thinning at resistance?
- Are sweeps coming through?
- Or is the offer refreshing (sellers defending)?

Offer refreshing at resistance = do not take the breakout.
