# ADHD Workflow

[[blaine]] has severe ADHD/OCD. This is not a side note — it is the dominant operational fact that shapes every system, tool, and process in this workspace.

## What this means in practice

- Everything competes for attention at once
- Staying organized and focused is the core daily struggle
- Complex systems create overhead that makes the problem worse, not better
- Solo operation means there is no one to catch dropped balls

## Design principles for this context

**Reduce friction, not increase it.** Every tool, habit, and system should lower the cognitive load, not raise it.

**Short loops.** Long multi-step workflows fall apart. Prefer: one action, one result, done.

**Visual clarity over density.** Less on screen is more. Cluttered dashboards are unusable.

**Defaults that work.** Can't rely on remembering to do setup steps every time. Automate or pre-configure wherever possible.

**Progress over perfection.** An imperfect system running beats a perfect system not built. Ship, then improve.

## How this shapes the AIOS

The AIOS goal is to reduce cognitive overhead — not add to it. That means:
- Short, direct answers (no fluff, no restating the question)
- Action-first responses — what needs doing, not status updates
- Flagging when something is about to become a decision, not burying it
- Keeping the workspace simple enough that Blaine can navigate it on a bad day

## What's working incrementally

Obsidian as a knowledge base. This wiki as a second brain. The AIOS as a system that handles the bookkeeping so [[blaine]] can focus on the actual work.
