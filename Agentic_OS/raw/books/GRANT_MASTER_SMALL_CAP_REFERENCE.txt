SECTION 1 --- TIM SYKES 7-STEP PENNYSTOCKING LIFECYCLE (STANDALONE REFERENCE)
==========================================================================================

Overview: The Sykes Lifecycle is the overarching pattern beneath every small cap momentum
move. It is not a single setup --- it is the full arc from accumulation to collapse.
All session setups in this document map to specific lifecycle stages.
Understanding WHERE in the lifecycle a stock sits changes everything about risk, edge,
and side (long vs. short).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 1 --- QUIET ACCUMULATION / BASE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: Stock is dormant. Low volume. Low volatility. Sleeper. Often trading below
prior highs for weeks or months. Float is thin, news is absent.

Characteristics:
- ADV below normal. Volume drying up. Price compressing.
- May sit near 52-week lows or in a range for days/weeks.
- Promoters, insiders, or early buyers accumulating quietly.
- No media, no alerts, no social activity.

Your role (long): Watch list only. No trade yet. Identify it early, add to PM scanner.
Your role (short): N/A. No edge yet.

Sykes Lifecycle Position: PRE-SETUP. This is stage 0 before the 7 stages fire.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 2 --- THE CATALYST / NEWS DROP
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: A real or manufactured catalyst hits. This ignites the move.
News drops pre-market, AH, or intraday. Volume explodes. Price gaps or spikes.

Top catalyst types that fire Stage 2 (ranked by average % move --- see Section 7):
1. FDA/PDUFA approval or clinical trial data (biotech --- +50% to +500% potential)
2. Government / DoD / DTRA contract award
3. Short squeeze trigger (SI > 15%, thin float + HOD break + halt)
4. Merger/acquisition announcement (premium to market)
5. Earnings beat with revenue acceleration
6. Licensing deal or major partnership
7. Sector hype / narrative alignment (AI, defense, clean energy, etc.)
8. Index inclusion (Russell 2000/3000 adds)
9. Reverse split with clean float reset (short-term only)
10. Social media viral / influencer mention (lowest quality / shortest duration)

Characteristics at Stage 2:
- Halt on first spike (L1 circuit breaker). This is normal and bullish on day 1.
- Pre-market volume 5x-50x avg daily volume.
- Price already up 20%-100%+ before 9:30am.
- Catalyst confirmed in press release or 8-K (not rumor).

Your role (long): This is FRONT-SIDE. Buy the first pullback post-halt.
Your role (short): DO NOT short yet. You will get squeezed. Locates will be gone.

Sykes Lifecycle Position: STAGE 2 --- CATALYST IGNITION.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 3 --- THE FIRST PARABOLIC RUN / SUPERNOVA
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: The stock makes its most explosive move. Multiple halts up.
Retail FOMO kicks in. Social media volume spikes. Shorts caught, covering hard.
This is the most violent phase for longs (biggest gains) and the most dangerous for shorts.

Characteristics:
- Multiple halt-resume sequences going UP (Level 2 circuit breakers).
- Volume is the highest it will be on this entire move.
- Price may triple or 10x in a single session (nano-cap, sub-5M float).
- Chart looks near-vertical. 1-minute chart is nearly parabolic.
- Bid/ask spread widens. Slippage brutal for latecomers.

Your role (long): FRONT-SIDE only. Enter early (Stage 2 entry or first post-halt pullback).
Sell INTO strength. Scale out before the parabola steepens.
Never chase into Stage 3 late. You become the bag for Stage 4 sellers.

Your role (short): Tempting but dangerous. Locates scarce or unavailable.
The ONLY legitimate short here is a FAILED HALT --- stock resumes and immediately breaks
opening range low of the halt candle. Otherwise, wait for Stage 4/5.

Sykes Lifecycle Position: STAGE 3 --- SUPERNOVA / PARABOLIC PEAK.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 4 --- THE CLIFF DIVE / FIRST RED DAY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: The stock reverses hard. Catalyst exhausted. Smart money distributed.
FOMO buyers from Stage 3 are now bag holders. Stock may drop 30%-70% in a session.

Characteristics:
- Gaps up or opens near HOD, then immediately fades below VWAP.
- Multiple halt-resume sequences going DOWN (L2 circuit breakers).
- Volume stays elevated but is now dominated by sellers.
- First meaningful red candle on 5-minute chart (Red-to-Green failed attempt).
- Opening range HIGH fails to hold. Stock cannot reclaim.

Your role (long): EXIT any remaining long. Do not hold through Stage 4.
One more push possible early (opening range push) --- scalp only with tight stop.

Your role (short): THIS IS THE PRIMARY SHORT ENTRY ZONE.
First Red Day setup initiates here. Entry criteria:
- Prior multi-day run of 300%+ (small cap) or 50%+ (large cap) already in.
- Day 1 (Stage 3) closed strong. Day 2 opens flat/up then fails.
- VWAP breakdown confirmed on 5-minute candle.
- Opening range low breaks.
- Stop at HOD or reclaim of VWAP.

Sykes Lifecycle Position: STAGE 4 --- FIRST RED DAY / CLIFF DIVE.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 5 --- THE DEAD CAT BOUNCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: After the cliff dive, price bounces. This traps second-wave buyers.
Shorts cover. Bag holders get relief. Sometimes bounces 20%-50% off the low.
This looks like the stock "recovering." It rarely does.

Characteristics:
- Price action looks constructive but volume is declining (churn).
- Every HOD made during Stage 5 is LOWER than the Stage 3 high.
- VWAP from the prior high day acts as resistance.
- Often coincides with social media wishful thinking ("it's coming back").

Your role (long): SCALP ONLY. Small size. Bounce target = 50% retrace of Stage 4 drop.
Must exit before VWAP from stage 3 day. Do not hold overnight expecting return to highs.

Your role (short): Re-enter short on VWAP or prior resistance rejection after the bounce.
Lower high confirmation = go again. Target prior Stage 4 low.

Sykes Lifecycle Position: STAGE 5 --- DEAD CAT / SECONDARY BOUNCE.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 6 --- SECONDARY LOWER HIGHS / GRIND DOWN
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: Multiple more bounce attempts, each failing at a lower level.
Volume declining each bounce. Momentum crowd exiting. ATM or shelf kicks in here.
Company often takes advantage of elevated price to sell shares.

Characteristics:
- Pattern of lower highs, lower lows.
- Volume shrinking on each bounce.
- Dilution risk actively materializing (ATM sales, shelf drawdown, toxic converts).
- Company may issue PR to support price (no substance).

Your role (long): NO TRADE. This is a short seller's playground, not a long entry zone.
Your role (short): Continue shorting bounces at overhead resistance / prior lows become
resistance. High-probability, lower volatility fades. Best risk/reward in the lifecycle.

Sykes Lifecycle Position: STAGE 6 --- SECONDARY FADES / DISTRIBUTION.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 7 --- DEATH SPIRAL / FADE TO IRRELEVANCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens: The stock fades entirely. Volume dries up. Price grinds toward zero or
gets delisted. Bag holders finally capitulate. Company may do reverse split to survive.

Characteristics:
- ADV returns to below pre-catalyst levels.
- No more halts. No more momentum.
- If biotech: failed trial may trigger, -90% in a session.
- If it was a pump: goes to zero quietly.

Your role: None. Move on. Do not trade dead tickers.

LIFECYCLE SUMMARY TABLE:
Stage 1 = Base = No trade
Stage 2 = Catalyst = Long front-side or watch
Stage 3 = Supernova = Long early / exit into strength
Stage 4 = Cliff dive = Short primary entry (First Red Day)
Stage 5 = Dead cat = Scalp long or re-short at VWAP rejection
Stage 6 = Lower highs = Short bounces, declining volatility
Stage 7 = Death = No trade

SECTION 2 --- PRE-MARKET MOMENTUM SCALPING (7:00AM - 9:28AM)
==========================================================================================
SESSION TAG: PRE-MARKET SCALP
SYKES LIFECYCLE MAPPING: Stage 2 Catalyst Ignition / Early Stage 3 Front-Side
TIME WINDOW: 7:00am - 9:28am (2 minutes before bell)
BEST FLOAT RANGE: Under 10M shares (nano to micro)
BEST PRICE RANGE: $1.00 - $20.00

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HIGHEST WIN RATE SETUP 1 --- PRE-MARKET HOD BREAKOUT (PMHOD)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock has been running in pre-market and approaches or sets a new pre-market
high on expanding volume with a confirmed catalyst.

Stock selection criteria:
- Float under 10M preferred (under 5M optimal)
- Pre-market volume at least 10x daily ADV before 8am
- Catalyst confirmed: actual PR, 8-K, or contract notice (not rumor)
- Price above all pre-market moving levels
- No active ATM / no equity line / no known offering priced above
- If previous day had a halt --- this is a continuation candidate (Stage 2/3)

Entry trigger: Price consolidates at or just below pre-market HOD for 2+ candles on the
2-minute chart, then breaks above with expanding volume on the next candle.
Confirmation = NEW pre-market HOD printed with volume above prior candle.

Stop: Below the consolidation base that formed under the PMHOD.
Target: Round number above, prior multi-day high, or next halt level if stock has halted.

Win rate context: Pre-market HOD breakouts with confirmed catalysts and thin floats
produce the highest win rates of any small cap setup --- estimated 60%-70%+ on A+ setups.
Key degrades: Catalyst quality drop, float expansion via offering, volume churn not expansion.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HIGHEST WIN RATE SETUP 2 --- PRE-MARKET VWAP RECLAIM (PM VWAP)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock gapped up on news, pulled back below PM VWAP, and is now reclaiming it.
This is a RED-TO-GREEN setup in the pre-market session.

Stock selection criteria:
- Same as above plus: initial spike already happened, pullback to VWAP confirmed
- Volume contracting during pullback (healthy)
- VWAP slope still pointing up

Entry trigger: Stock reclaims PM VWAP on a 2-minute candle close above with volume expansion.
Hold: Look for the candle to stay above VWAP for at least 1 full 2-minute bar.

Stop: Below the PM low of the pullback (not below VWAP --- below the swing low).
Target: Retest of PM HOD, then prior day high if still open gap.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HIGHEST WIN RATE SETUP 3 --- PRE-MARKET LOD REVERSAL (PM LOD REVERSAL)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock dropped hard in early pre-market and is showing signs of exhaustion /
bottom formation at key support. Rare in true momentum plays --- more common in sympathy
names or stocks with late-breaking positive news reversing early selling.

Stock selection criteria:
- Stock already declined 15%+ from pre-market high
- Volume spiking on reversal candle (capitulation)
- Catalyst still intact (news hasn't been retracted)
- No dilution event pending (offering priced below current level, for example)

Entry trigger: First green 2-minute candle after 2+ consecutive red candles at LOD.
Volume on the green candle must exceed the prior red candle.

Stop: Below the LOD by 3-5 cents (tight on nano-cap, wider on higher-priced names).
Target: 50% retrace to pre-market high, then VWAP.

Win rate note: LOD reversals have LOWER win rates than HOD breakouts pre-market.
Use reduced size. Best in biotech names where catalyst fundamentally revalues the stock.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PRE-MARKET REGIME CHECK (DO BEFORE EVERY TRADE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Is the catalyst CONFIRMED (PR/8-K/EDGAR)? If not --- B or C grade maximum.
2. Has the stock already halted? If yes: post-halt first candle volume expansion required.
3. Is the float clean? Run Dilution Tracker check. Any active S-3 shelf? ATM? = reduce size.
4. Is there a competitor name or sympathy play in the same sector? Splits momentum.
5. What is the broad market doing? (SPY/QQQ pre-market direction filters regime.)
6. Is this a brand new name or a known recycler (stock that has run 3+ times before)?
   Known recyclers have lower continuation rates and higher dilution probability.

SECTION 3 --- MARKET OPEN DAY TRADING (9:30AM - 11:30AM)
==========================================================================================
SESSION TAG: OPEN DAY TRADE
SYKES LIFECYCLE MAPPING: Stage 3 Supernova (early) / Stage 2 Continuation / Stage 4 (short)
TIME WINDOW: 9:30am - 11:30am
BEST FLOAT RANGE: Under 15M for momentum, 5M-50M for VWAP / structure plays
BEST PRICE RANGE: $1.00 - $50.00

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPEN SESSION SETUP 1 --- HOD BREAKOUT AT OPEN (HIGHEST WIN RATE IN SESSION)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Pre-market high holds as support through the open. Stock consolidates 9:30-9:45
then breaks above PMHOD on the first major volume impulse.

Best stock characteristics:
- Float under 10M
- Pre-market gap up 20%+ on real news
- PMHOD holding as support through 9:30-9:45 consolidation
- Market regime: risk-on (SPY green, QQQ green, no broad selloff)
- No offering / no shelf draw below current price

Opening range rule: Define opening range as the HIGH and LOW of the first 5 or 15-minute
candle. HOD breakout setup fires when price breaks the 5-minute high of the opening candle
with volume at least 1.5x the opening candle's volume.

Entry: Break above the 5-minute opening candle high (or PMHOD if higher).
Stop: Below the low of the consolidation before the break (not the opening candle low ---
the most recent swing low within the 9:30-9:45 range).
Target 1: Prior multi-day high or round number above.
Target 2: Next halt zone if stock has been halting up.

Win rate context: Opening HOD breakouts with catalyst + thin float + expanding volume on
break are the single highest win rate intraday setup in small caps. Win rates on A+ criteria
can reach 65%-75%. Most failures come from: lack of catalyst, float already expanded by
dilution, or broad market sell off killing risk appetite.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPEN SESSION SETUP 2 --- RED-TO-GREEN REVERSAL (9:30AM - 10:00AM WINDOW)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock opens in the red (below prior day close or below VWAP from prior day),
then makes a decisive reclaim of that level on volume.

Best stock characteristics:
- Float under 15M
- Stock was a prior day runner that is retesting / consolidating
- No new negative catalyst (dilution news, failed trial, etc.)
- Broad market neutral to green
- Volume on the green reclaim candle must exceed the prior red candle (not just equal)

Entry: Candle CLOSES above prior day close or above intraday VWAP with volume expansion.
Do not enter on a wick above --- wait for the full candle close.
Stop: Below the LOD at the time of entry.
Target 1: Prior day HOD. Target 2: Multi-week highs if structure allows.

Note: Red-to-Green in the first 30 minutes carries higher win rate than the same setup
later in the day. The open brings the most institutional and algorithmic order flow,
making VWAP levels more respected and level breaks more decisive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPEN SESSION SETUP 3 --- VWAP RECLAIM (9:45AM - 11:00AM WINDOW)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock broke VWAP early in the session, pulled below, then reclaims it with
a higher low structure forming below the VWAP.

Key distinction: This is NOT the same as a stock flailing above and below VWAP repeatedly.
A valid VWAP reclaim requires:
1. One clear break below VWAP (not just a wick --- a candle CLOSE below)
2. A clear pullback and higher low formed BELOW VWAP
3. The reclaim candle closes ABOVE VWAP with volume expansion (not on low volume)
4. VWAP is sloping UP at the time of reclaim

Entry: Candle close above VWAP on the reclaim.
Stop: Below the higher low that formed before the reclaim.
Target 1: PMHOD or prior HOD. Target 2: Next structural resistance.

Best in: Stocks that already have a clear catalyst and opened strong but pulled back
normally (profit-taking, not structural failure). The higher low is critical --- it
signals buyers defending the pullback zone.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPEN SESSION SETUP 4 --- FIRST PULLBACK (MOMENTUM CONTINUATION)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: A strong trending stock has its first consolidation candle (red candle or
tight range candle) after an extended run from the open. Entry on the next break to HOD.

Best in: Momentum runners that are clearly Stage 3 (multiple green candles, each new HOD).
Criteria: First red candle on 5-minute chart. Candle must hold above VWAP or prior HOD.
Entry: Break above the red candle high on next candle.
Stop: Below the low of the first pullback candle.
Target: Measured move (height of prior move from VWAP to HOD, projected up).

Win rate note: FIRST pullback only. Second, third, fourth pullbacks have dramatically lower
win rates as the front-side momentum exhausts. If you missed the first pull, skip the setup.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPEN SESSION RISK FRAMEWORK
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Volume quality hierarchy (highest to lowest win rate):
1. Volume expansion on breakout candle (buying aggression confirmed)
2. Volume contraction during pullback + expansion on next HOD break (healthy rhythm)
3. Flat volume on break (uncertain --- reduce size)
4. Volume spike with no price progression (absorption --- likely supply --- exit)

Time of day risk degradation:
9:30 - 10:00 = Maximum edge. Highest institutional order flow. Highest win rate.
10:00 - 11:00 = Strong edge. Most setups still valid. Slightly more chop.
11:00 - 11:30 = Declining edge. Setup quality drops. Use smaller size or skip unless A+.
11:30 and beyond = See Section 4 (Afternoon Swing). Different rules apply.

Key levels that matter MOST at the open (ranked by importance):
1. Pre-market HOD (PM session high) --- most watched by momentum traders
2. Prior day close --- institutional baseline, triggers algo buy/sell programs
3. Prior day HOD --- first structural resistance for continuation
4. VWAP (live, intraday) --- institutional fair value benchmark
5. Opening range high/low (first 5 or 15 min) --- the battle range
6. Round numbers ($1, $2, $5, $10, $20, $50) --- crowd psychology magnets

SECTION 4 --- AFTERNOON SWING SETUPS / GAP UP SELECTION (12PM - 4PM)
==========================================================================================
SESSION TAG: AFTERNOON SWING / OVERNIGHT / 1-3 DAY
SYKES LIFECYCLE MAPPING: Stage 2/3 Continuation (overnight) / Stage 5 Dead Cat (AH bounce)
TIME WINDOW: 12:00pm - 4:00pm (entry zone)
HOLD TOLERANCE: Flexible --- Overnight only / 1-3 day / Into catalyst event

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TIER 1 --- OVERNIGHT HOLD (HIGHEST WIN RATE OF THE THREE TIERS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Target: Gap up next morning. 1-day hold maximum.
Best setup: Close near HOD (within 5% of HOD) with catalyst still fresh.

Selection criteria (all must pass):
1. Catalyst is ACTIVE and NOT fully priced in (new news in last 24 hours)
2. Float under 15M (the thinner the better for overnight continuation)
3. Stock closed in the top 25% of day's range (near HOD, not mid-range)
4. Volume on the close session was EXPANDING (not contracting --- not just churning)
5. No dilution event announced or expected in next 24 hours
6. Day 1 of a multi-day move (Stage 2-3, not Stage 5 dead cat)
7. No earnings announcement before next open
8. Short interest elevated but not extreme (SI 10%-30% = squeeze fuel)
   Very high SI (30%+) adds squeeze risk but also halting risk --- reduce size
9. Broad market neutral to bullish heading into close

Best sectors for overnight gap continuation:
- Biotech (PDUFA next day, trial readout pending, contract win same day)
- Defense / Government contracts (hard catalysts, no dilution typical)
- AI / Tech plays with institutional validation (named partnership / contract)
- Short squeeze situations (high SI + thin float + catalyst + HOD close)

Target on next day open: Pre-market HOD break from close zone.
Stop on next day open: Below prior day close (rule-based, not emotional).

Win rate context: Day 1 close near HOD + thin float + real catalyst + clean dilution profile
= estimated 60%+ continuation rate to a higher next-day open in active market regimes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TIER 2 --- 1-3 DAY HOLD
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Target: Multi-day momentum continuation. Stage 3 extended run.
Best setup: Bull flag or tight consolidation on daily chart after a Day 1 catalyst move.

Selection criteria (all must pass):
1. Day 1 move was 30%+ and closed above VWAP (not a spike and fade)
2. Day 2-3 shows tight consolidation (range under 10% of Day 1 move) on DECREASING volume
3. No new negative catalyst
4. Float still thin (no major offering between Day 1 and now)
5. Pattern: Bull flag, pennant, or flat top consolidation below prior HOD
6. Anchored VWAP from Day 1 catalyst is being respected as support

Entry: Day 2 or 3 breakout above the consolidation high with volume expansion.
Stop: Below the consolidation low.
Target: Measured move from Day 1 base to HOD, projected from breakout point.
Trail: Trail stop under each new higher low once in profit.

Risk note: Multi-day holds dramatically increase dilution exposure. Re-run EDGAR check
each morning. Companies routinely file S-3 supplements or ATM drawdowns after price spikes.
If any offering surfaces --- exit the swing immediately, do not hold through.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TIER 3 --- INTO CATALYST EVENT (HIGHEST RISK / HIGHEST REWARD)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Target: Pre-catalyst run-up, then exit BEFORE the event (not through it).
Best setup: Biotech with known PDUFA date, earnings with whisper, contract announcement pending.

Selection criteria (all must pass):
1. Catalyst date known and confirmed (PDUFA date, earnings date, contract decision date)
2. Stock has been making higher lows leading into the event (run-up pattern)
3. Volume increasing daily on the run-up (distribution not yet happening)
4. Float thin or short interest elevated (squeeze potential if event is positive)
5. Risk is BINARY --- you MUST exit before the event unless you are taking a specific thesis
   trade on the outcome (not a momentum scalp)

Strategy: BUY the run-up. SELL INTO the event. Let someone else hold the binary.
Exit rule: Exit minimum 1 hour before catalyst event. Never hold through an FDA decision
or binary clinical trial result as a momentum trader --- the downside is -50% to -90%
and no stop loss protects you from an after-hours gap on a failed trial.

Exception: If you have done fundamental diligence and are taking a directional bet on the
outcome --- size appropriately (1-2% of account maximum) and accept full loss potential.

SECTION 5 --- AFTER-HOURS STOCK SELECTION (HIGHEST CONTINUATION %)
==========================================================================================
SESSION TAG: AFTER-HOURS / CONTINUATION
SYKES LIFECYCLE MAPPING: Stage 2/3 AH catalyst / Overnight continuation qualifier
TIME WINDOW: 4:00pm - 8:00pm

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AH SETUP 1 --- CATALYST-DRIVEN AH SPIKE (HIGHEST CONTINUATION RATE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Company releases material news post-close (earnings, contract, FDA, partnership).
Stock spikes 20%+ in AH. The question is whether it continues next morning.

Continuation rate is highest when:
1. AH volume is at least 3x-5x the stock's normal full-day volume
2. News is FUNDAMENTAL and HARD (not promotional --- actual revenue, approval, contract)
3. AH price action consolidates ABOVE the spike level (tight range, not fading)
4. The stock is not a known serial dilutor (check EDGAR before close next morning)
5. Float under 20M
6. Short interest elevated (short covering will fuel next morning gap continuation)

Watch for in AH:
- Does the initial spike HOLD? If yes = strong continuation signal.
- Does it FADE back toward close = weak. Pre-market may not gap further.
- Does it go VERTICAL with no pause? = Likely short-lived. Halts may occur at open.

AH monitoring levels to mark:
- AH HOD (will act as resistance or breakout target at open)
- AH LOD (key support; if broken next morning, continuation thesis is dead)
- AH VWAP (institutional fair value for the AH session)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AH SETUP 2 --- AH HALT/RESUME CONTINUATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock halts in AH (Level 3 or news halt) then resumes higher.
This is the HIGHEST continuation signal in AH because it indicates institutional
buying is so aggressive that exchanges had to pause to clear the imbalance.

Criteria: Resume after halt is AT OR ABOVE prior halt level.
If resume is significantly below halt level = exhaustion signal, not continuation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AH CONTINUATION KILLER SIGNALS (EXIT OVERNIGHT HOLD IF THESE APPEAR)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Company files S-3 supplement or prospectus in AH or early pre-market
2. AH HOD is selling LOWER HIGHS (each AH high is lower than prior)
3. Volume in AH is extremely low (spike was on thin liquidity, not real demand)
4. A competing news story or sympathy name is stealing AH momentum
5. Sector turns against overnight (e.g., FDA issues a sector-wide warning)
6. Stock is already up 300%+ over prior 5 days and catalyst is not transformational

SECTION 6 --- TOP-DOWN KEY LEVELS: WHAT RETAIL AND INSTITUTIONS WATCH
==========================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
INSTITUTIONAL LEVELS (MOST RESPECTED BY ALGORITHMS AND LARGE PLAYERS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These levels are NOT just technical. They are execution benchmarks for large players.
Knowing where institutions execute = knowing where supply and demand walls form.

Rank 1 --- VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price --- intraday daily anchor)
What it is: The single most important intraday level watched by every institutional
algorithm. Institutions benchmark order fills against VWAP. Algos are programmed to
buy below VWAP and sell into VWAP. THIS IS THE LEVEL THAT MATTERS MOST intraday.

Rules for reading VWAP:
- Price above VWAP = bulls in control = bias long
- Price below VWAP = bears in control = bias short
- VWAP reclaim on volume = momentum shift = buy signal
- VWAP rejection after test = continued downtrend = short entry or exit longs
- VWAP in choppy / low-volume sessions = neutral pivot, not a trend signal

Rank 2 --- ANCHORED VWAP (From catalyst date or major pivot)
Used to identify: where institutional buyers are still "in the money" from a major event.
Anchor from: Day 1 catalyst candle. Any major high or low that preceded a large move.
When price returns to anchored VWAP = institutional cost basis defense / give-up zone.

Rank 3 --- PRE-MARKET HOD/LOD (Session-specific)
Pre-market high = THE primary resistance / breakout target for momentum traders at open.
Pre-market low = The floor. Break of PM low = significant bearish signal.
Every serious momentum scanner and day trader has PM high/low marked before 9:30am.

Rank 4 --- PRIOR DAY CLOSE
Institutional baseline. Program trading often triggers at this level.
Red-to-Green (reclaim of prior day close) is the most universally recognized
momentum signal in small caps. Short sellers often cover on this reclaim.

Rank 5 --- PRIOR DAY HOD/LOD
First structural resistance / support above/below the current day's action.
Breakout of prior day HOD on volume = first confirmation of continuation.
Break of prior day LOD = structural breakdown = long thesis dead.

Rank 6 --- ROUND NUMBERS / PSYCHOLOGICAL LEVELS
$1.00, $2.00, $5.00, $10.00, $20.00, $50.00, $100.00
These are crowd psychology magnets. NOT coincidental. Shorts place hard stops here.
Breakouts above round numbers bring in new retail buyers (FOMO trigger).
Round numbers also act as warrant strike clustering zones on many small caps.

Rank 7 --- WEEKLY VWAP (Rolling weekly average)
Watched by swing traders and some institutions for multi-day trend context.
Price above weekly VWAP = multi-day bullish bias.
Price below weekly VWAP = multi-day bearish bias.

Rank 8 --- 9 EMA / 20 EMA (5-minute chart)
Not institutional per se, but universally used by momentum retail traders.
9 EMA = fastest trend line. Stock holding above 9 EMA on 5-minute = trending up.
Break below 9 EMA with volume = first warning of trend change on intraday.
20 EMA = first real support in a healthy intraday uptrend.
Price below BOTH 9 and 20 EMA and rejecting them from below = full bear control.

Rank 9 --- DAILY 20 EMA and 50 SMA
Multi-day support for swing holds. Institutions defend these in trending names.
Small caps breaking above 20 EMA daily on heavy volume = swing long entry zone.
Price below 50 SMA daily = swing short bias or avoid longs entirely.

Rank 10 --- FIBONACCI RETRACEMENTS (38.2%, 50%, 61.8%)
Used by retail swing traders and some algorithmic systems for pullback entries.
38.2% = shallow pullback, strong trend --- buy here on volume confirmation
50% = moderate pullback, most common bounce zone
61.8% = deep pullback, final support before trend invalidation

SECTION 7 --- CATALYST KEYWORDS RANKED BY AVERAGE MOVE SIZE
==========================================================================================
Rankings are based on observed average post-announcement move in small cap names
(under $2B market cap). All session types covered.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TIER 1 --- TRANSFORMATIONAL CATALYSTS (Avg move: +50% to +500%+)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are the HIGHEST-MAGNITUDE catalysts. One of these on a sub-5M float name can produce
a 10x same-day move. These fire Stage 2-3 of the Sykes Lifecycle.

Keyword categories:
FDA Approval / PDUFA Decision Positive --- "FDA grants approval" / "NDA approved"

SECTION 9 --- WHERE SHORTS HAVE HIGHEST WIN RATE VS. LONGS
==========================================================================================
This section maps the specific conditions where SHORT-SIDE trades have a structural edge
over long-side trades. Critical for trap avoidance and for knowing when to flip.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHORT SETUP 1 --- FIRST RED DAY (HIGHEST WIN RATE SHORT IN SMALL CAPS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sykes Lifecycle mapping: Stage 3 to 4 transition.

When shorts WIN here:
- Stock already up 300%+ (small cap) or 50%+ (large cap) over prior 3-5 days
- Day of short is the FIRST day it opens flat or slightly up THEN FAILS
- Opening range high cannot be taken out (rejected at open)
- VWAP from prior days acts as resistance (anchored VWAP reference)
- Volume on the opening push is DECLINING vs. prior days (exhaustion)
- Stock cannot halt UP in the first 30 minutes despite trying (energy failing)

Entry: Short the break of the opening range LOW of the short session.
Alternative entry: Short the VWAP rejection after the bounce off LOD.
Stop: HOD of the current short session. If HOD is taken out = thesis dead.
Target: 30%-40% below the short session open. Prior multi-day support. Anchored VWAP.

Win rate context: First Red Day on qualitatively strong setups (multi-day run, thin float,
confirmed exhaustion) produces some of the highest win rates in all of small cap trading.
Estimated 65%-75% win rate on A-tier First Red Day setups.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHORT SETUP 2 --- PARABOLIC EXHAUSTION SHORT (INTRADAY)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock accelerates VERTICALLY on the 5-minute chart, forming nearly vertical
candles. This signals final-stage buying (FOMO buyers chasing, shorts covering).

When shorts WIN here:
- Prior upside move is +100% to +1000% for small cap in current session / multi-session
- 5-minute candle bodies getting LARGER with each new high (acceleration = exhaustion)
- Volume exploding on vertical candles (climactic buying = distribution)
- Gap on current candle is large (exhaustion gap)
- Stock cannot hold the open of the current largest candle for 2+ candles after

Entry: Short the VWAP breakdown (5-minute candle close below VWAP) after the parabolic
top candle. If missed: Short the break of the opening range low of the top candle.
Stop: Current day HOD. Tight on high-quality setups.
Target: -30% to -40% from entry. VWAP from prior day. Measured move of the parabola.

Key rule: Small caps up 300%+ can correct 40%-70% in a single session.
Large caps up 50%+ tend to correct 10%-20% in a session.
Scale expectations to the magnitude of the prior run.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHORT SETUP 3 --- DEAD CAT FADE (STAGE 5 RE-SHORT)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it is: Stock that has already done Stage 4 cliff dive bounces to VWAP or prior
resistance. The bounce is on declining volume. This is the re-entry short.

When shorts WIN here:
- Price bounces to VWAP from Stage 4 close
- Volume on the bounce is DECLINING (weak hands covering, not new buyers)
- VWAP from Stage 3 high day acts as resistance (anchored VWAP resistance)
- HOD of the bounce is LOWER than Stage 3 HOD (lower high confirmed)
- 2+ consecutive red candles after the bounce peak

Entry: Short the failure at VWAP / prior support-turned-resistance.
Stop: Above bounce HOD.
Target: Stage 4 LOD, then measured move below.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHERE SHORTS LOSE --- TRAP ZONES FOR SHORT SELLERS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Understanding where shorts lose = understanding where longs WIN (and vice versa).

Short sellers LOSE in these conditions:
1. Shorting BEFORE halts on a front-side momentum name (Stage 2/3)
   The halt mechanism traps shorts who enter too early. The stock gaps above their stops.
   Rule: Never short a halting stock before it shows exhaustion (failed halt resume).

2. Shorting VWAP reclaim on a stock with confirmed catalyst
   If the news is real and the float is thin, shorts who fade VWAP reclaims get squeezed.
   Rule: Don't short VWAP reclaims on Day 1 catalyst plays with thin float.

3. Shorting into a known short squeeze (High SI + thin float + catalyst combined)
   The squeeze can run 10x before exhausting. Locates vanish. Borrow cost destroys P&L.
   Rule: Never short a stock with SI above 30% on Day 1 of a catalyst. Wait for Stage 4/5.

4. Shorting after a multi-day downtrend at LOD (Stage 6/7 bottom fade)
   Stocks in Stage 6/7 can bounce violently when dilution clears. Short at LOD is trapped.
   Rule: Fade bounces in Stage 6, not new lows.

5. Shorting based only on fundamentals (company is "bad")
   Bad companies can run 500% in a week on hype before the fundamentals matter.
   Rule: Short the TECHNICALS and LIFECYCLE STAGE, not the fundamentals alone.

SECTION 10 --- DILUTION EVENTS RANKED BY DANGER LEVEL
==========================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DANGER TIER 1 --- MAXIMUM THREAT (Hard Avoid on any long thesis)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These events create immediate, severe, and often irreversible downward pressure.
No long position should be held through confirmed active Tier 1 events.

1. TOXIC CONVERTIBLE NOTES / VARIABLE RATE CONVERTS
   What it is: Debt that converts to shares at a DISCOUNT to market price.
   The lower the stock goes, the more shares the note converts to.
   Classic "death spiral" financing. The lender is actively incentivized to short the stock.
   Detection: EDGAR --- 8-K for convertible note with variable or floating conversion rate.
   Keywords to search in filing: "variable conversion," "lowest trading price," "discount to VWAP"
   Action: Hard Avoid. No long under any circumstances while active.

2. EQUITY LINE OF CREDIT (ELOC) / COMMITTED EQUITY FACILITY
   What it is: Company can sell shares to a single buyer (often a predatory fund) on demand
   over time. The buyer ALWAYS hedges by shorting. Creates permanent downward supply.
   Detection: 8-K for "Equity Distribution Agreement" / "Committed Equity Facility"
   Action: Hard Avoid.

3. ACTIVE ATM WITH KNOWN ACTIVE SALES ABOVE CURRENT PRICE
   What it is: At-the-market program with confirmed ongoing share sales into volume spikes.
   Detection: Most recent 10-Q or prospectus supplement showing shares sold under ATM.
   Action: Hard Avoid for sustained holds. Scalp only with immediate exit on volume dry-up.

4. S-1 REGISTRATION OF PIPE SHARES AT A DISCOUNT TO CURRENT PRICE
   What it is: PIPE investors received shares at a discount (e.g., $0.50 when stock is $2.00).
   When the S-1 or S-3 becomes effective, those investors can immediately sell.
   Effective date = Supply wall unlocked. Price often drops to the PIPE price level.
   Detection: S-1/S-3 filing for "resale of shares" --- check the conversion/issue price.
   Action: Hard Avoid after registration becomes effective until supply absorbed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DANGER TIER 2 --- HIGH THREAT (Reduce size / limit hold time / tighter stops)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These create meaningful downward pressure but may not be immediate.
Manageable with strict position sizing and defined exit rules.

5. ACTIVE ATM WITH NO RECENT CONFIRMED SALES
   Active S-3 shelf + registered ATM but no evidence of active selling in latest filing.
   The CAPACITY for dilution is live but not confirmed as actively selling right now.
   Threshold trigger: ATM capacity exceeds 20% of current float = meaningful ceiling.
   Action: Reduce size to 50% of normal. Exit on any sign of volume dry-up after a pop.

6. WARRANTS WITH STRIKE PRICE WITHIN 10-20% OF CURRENT PRICE
   Warrant holders are incentivized to exercise if stock is near or above strike.
   Exercise creates new shares = supply. Strike price acts as a STRUCTURAL CEILING.
   Common in: PIPE-financed companies, recent IPOs, reverse merger shells.
   Detection: Latest 10-Q or prospectus for "warrant exercise price" table.
   Action: Recognize the strike as a resistance level. Do not hold target above warrant strike
   without confirming strike-level ask is not refreshing.

7. S-3 SHELF WITH LARGE CAPACITY RELATIVE TO MARKET CAP
   Large shelf = option for company to raise capital at any time.
   If shelf capacity exceeds 25% of market cap = high threat to price.
   Not an immediate trigger but signals company is planning future dilution.
   Action: Treat any spike as potential selling opportunity for the company.

8. UNDERWRITTEN OFFERING (TRADITIONAL OR RDO)
   Company announces new share issuance through underwriters.
   Classic response: Gap down to the offering price. Offering price = new near-term floor.
   Duration of pressure: 1-5 trading days post-announcement.
   Exception: Oversubscribed offering, no warrants, premium pricing = SHORT-TERM bullish.
   Action: Avoid long through offering announcement. Buy after price stabilizes at offering price.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DANGER TIER 3 --- MODERATE THREAT (Be aware / no immediate action required)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are real but slower-acting risks that don't create immediate supply walls.

9. AUTHORIZED SHARE INCREASE (PENDING OR APPROVED)
   Company seeks shareholder approval to increase authorized shares.
   Not immediate dilution --- just removes the structural cap on how many shares can be issued.
   Action: Note it. Combined with shelf or ATM = elevated threat. Alone = background risk.

10. WARRANTS WITH STRIKE PRICE 50%+ ABOVE CURRENT PRICE (FAR OUT OF THE MONEY)
    These warrants have almost no near-term dilution pressure.
    Holders cannot profitably exercise. Not a ceiling at current prices.
    Action: Track them but don't penalize the trade. Monitor if stock approaches strike.

11. STOCK-BASED COMPENSATION (EMPLOYEE/DIRECTOR GRANTS)
    Dilutive over time but not a significant near-term supply pressure.
    Most employee grants have vesting schedules and lockup periods.
    Action: Note in background analysis. Not a trade filter.

12. SHELF REGISTRATION WITH SMALL CAPACITY (UNDER 10% OF MARKET CAP)
    Company has the ability to raise but limited capacity.
    Low near-term threat unless they activate it with an offering announcement.
    Action: No immediate impact. Monitor for S-3 supplement filing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHEN DILUTION IS PRICED IN / NOT A THREAT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These conditions suggest dilution risk is reduced or irrelevant to current setup:

1. Offering was announced AND priced ALREADY, stock stabilized at offering price.
   The supply is known and absorbed. New buyers are post-dilution buyers.
   The stock often makes its next momentum move UP from the offering price once absorbed.

2. ATM is EXHAUSTED (all registered shares under ATM have been sold).
   Check: Latest prospectus supplement shows "all shares sold" or ATM terminated.
   If ATM capacity is fully used, the ongoing supply pressure ends.

3. Warrants are EXPIRED or exercise window has closed.
   Check EDGAR for warrant expiration dates. Expired warrants = no more ceiling.

4. Convertible note has been FULLY CONVERTED to shares.
   Once converted: principal is gone, no more conversion dilution.
   Check: Latest 10-Q for "converted in full" note disclosures.

5. STRONG CATALYST OVERRIDES DILUTION TEMPORARILY (short-term window).
   A Tier 1 catalyst (FDA approval, DoD contract) can override active dilution pressure
   for a SHORT WINDOW (1 session to 3 days) as new demand overwhelms supply.
   Rule: Use this window to TRADE, not to HOLD. Exit before the dilution resumes.

6. Clean balance sheet + no financing need.
   Company has sufficient cash runway (12+ months confirmed).
   Has no active shelf, no convertibles, no ATM, no ELOC.
   This is the ideal long candidate. Prioritize these names when found.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DILUTION THRESHOLD TRIGGERS (EXACT NUMBERS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are specific numeric thresholds that change the threat level in real-time:

- ATM capacity > 20% of current float = High Threat (Tier 2 escalates to Tier 1 territory)
- Warrant overhang > 30% of current float = Structural ceiling, hard avoid above strike
- Authorized shares > 3x outstanding shares = Company has significant dilution runway
- Cash runway < 6 months + active shelf = Near-certain capital raise incoming
- Session volume > 5x reported OS = Float is wrong, real-time issuance likely active
- Reverse split count in trailing 12 months = 3 or more = Hard Avoid permanently
- PIPE discount > 30% below current market = S-1/S-3 effective date = supply wall incoming
- Offering priced below 90-day VWAP = Institutional confidence absent = avoid
- Convertible note principal > 20% of market cap = Dilution risk severe
