PLAYBOOK OVERVIEW

Ross Cameron's strategy is built on a single foundational premise: identify stocks that are already moving, ride the momentum on the breakout, and get out fast. Unlike contrarian or dip-buying approaches, Ross explicitly buys strength — he enters when a stock makes new highs with high volume and exits before momentum fades.

His method is long-biased, scalp-oriented, and applied strictly to low-float small-cap stocks. It does not use complex indicators. It relies on a repeatable 5-pillar stock-selection framework, pattern recognition on 1-minute and 5-minute charts, and disciplined mechanical exits.

Core Principle (confirmed, direct): "I'm not interested in stocks that might move, are 'primed' to move, or are 'significantly undervalued.' I only focus on stocks that are already moving." — Ross Cameron, How To Day Trade: The Plain Truth

  4. Enter on the first candle to make a new high with volume confirmation.

Section 03 · Setup Catalog Ross Cameron · Warrior Trading

SETUP CATALOG

Ross trades a focused catalog of momentum-breakout setups. All share the same operating logic: buy new highs on volume, confirm with chart pattern, exit before momentum stalls.

SETUP 1: GAP AND GO
Core Setup · Long · Opening Drive

Concept

A stock gaps up significantly pre-market on news catalyst. At the open, it continues to push higher without filling the gap. Ross identifies these as the "leading gapper" each morning and trades the continuation of that gap-up energy.

The logic is simple: if buyers were aggressive enough pre-market to create a large gap, they are likely to continue buying at the open. The gap itself signals urgency — a catalyst strong enough that traders couldn't wait for the regular session.

Key Quote (confirmed, Medium): "It's about seizing the initiative from the get-go by capitalizing on stocks that have gapped up at market open."

Rules

  • Gap must be 10%+ pre-market with news catalyst
  • Float <10M, price $2–$20, RVOL 5x+
  • At 9:30, watch for continuation above pre-market high
  • Do NOT buy if it gaps and immediately fades (failed gapper)
  • Bull flag consolidation near pre-market high = ideal entry setup
  • Entry: breakout above pre-market high on 1-min candle close

Why This Works

  • Short sellers who faded pre-market are caught wrong
  • FOMO retail traders pile in at open, fueling continuation
  • Low float means small volume = large % moves
Entry Trigger
First candle to make new high after consolidation. Confirmed with high volume on 1-min chart. Limit order on the ask using ARCA/NASDAQ routing.
Stop Logic
Mental stop 10–20 cents below entry. Low of consolidation candle / flag. "Breakout or Bailout" — if it doesn't move immediately, exit. Do NOT let small loss become large loss.
Target / Exit
Sell 50% at first profit target (2:1). Adjust stop to breakeven. Sell additional pieces on the ask. Final exit on first red 5-min candle or level 2 showing big seller.
Confidence
High — Named as primary strategy. Confirmed in course materials, book, Medium articles, Warrior Trading website. Most referenced setup in public record.
SETUP 2: BULL FLAG BREAKOUT
Core Setup · 1-Min & 5-Min · Highest Volume of Data

Concept

The bull flag is Ross's most statistically documented setup — he has taken 24,000+ trades and reports ~69% accuracy on this pattern specifically. It consists of a strong initial move (the flagpole), followed by a low-volume pullback (the flag), and then a breakout to new highs (the continuation).

The flag is the key: low-volume red candles during the consolidation signal weak selling — profit-takers, not sellers. When volume re-enters on the green breakout candle, that's the entry signal.

Anatomy of the Setup

  • Flagpole: Big green candle on high volume — initial spike
  • Flag: Sideways consolidation with 2–4 low-volume red candles
  • Entry: First candle that makes a new high — breakout of the flag
  • Max loss: Low of the pullback (bottom of the flag)
  • Target: Height of flagpole projected from breakout point
  • Best stocks: $2–$20, 10%+ day gain, 5x RVOL minimum
Confirmed — Warrior Trading website, direct Ross Cameron article, 24,000+ trade data, ~69% accuracy stated directly
Volume Signal
HIGH volume on green flagpole candles. LIGHT volume on red consolidation candles. Volume surge on breakout candle = confirmation. Inverse = avoid.
Entry Precision
Entry at first candle to make new high. Level 2 must show buyers on the ask, not heavy sellers. If L2 shows big seller, wait or skip.
Scaling Out Rules
Sell 50% on ask when first target touched. Adjust stop to breakeven. Sell more on 2nd/3rd targets. Bail on bid if L2 shows resistance or tape turns red.
Failure Mode
Flag breaks below consolidation low. Stock fails to make new high. Volume on red candles exceeds green candles. "If it doesn't move immediately, I exit." — confirmed direct quote.
SETUP 3: VWAP BREAKOUT & BOUNCE
Intraday

Ross uses VWAP as a key dynamic level. VWAP Breakout: Stock trades under VWAP (bears in control), accumulating short sellers. When it breaks above, shorts are forced to cover — creating a powerful upside move. VWAP Bounce: Stock already above VWAP, pulls back to test it, holds — bulls confirmed.

  • Entry: Bull flag at VWAP with stop just below VWAP support
  • Target: Retest of high of day
  • Breakout setup: Stock under VWAP + sudden break upward = parabolic potential
  • Confirmed: "Several of the biggest winning trades I've taken have been based on the VWAP breakout" — direct quote
Confirmed — Warrior Trading VWAP article, direct Ross Cameron statement
SETUP 4: ABCD PATTERN
Reversal · Multi-Leg

A 4-point pattern where A→B is the initial move, B→C is the pullback, and C→D is the continuation. Ross enters at the D-point breakout — when the stock resumes its upward move after the BC correction. This is a higher-timeframe version of the bull flag logic.

  • Entry trigger: D-point breakout with volume confirmation
  • Stop: Below C-point (support of BC pullback)
  • Target: Equal to A→B distance projected from D
  • Best used mid-morning after initial gap-and-go has played out
Confirmed — Named in Warrior Trading curriculum, scanner documentation, and course materials
SETUP 5: MULTI-DAY CONTINUATION
Swing-Day Hybrid · Higher Duration

A stock surges 30–100% on a news catalyst and continues climbing over the following days — culminating in a final short squeeze. Ross trades the subsequent day's continuation, not just the first day's gap. The key requirement is that the stock holds support overnight and gaps again or opens strong.

  • Typical duration: 2–5 days of continuation potential
  • Final phase: parabolic move + short squeeze = highest risk/reward
  • Exit criteria identical to intraday — sell into strength on red candle signals

Why It Works

Shorts who missed the initial move try to fade the extended run. As the stock holds and continues, their positions are squeezed, adding buying pressure. RVOL remains elevated because media attention and retail interest continues for days after the initial catalyst.

Confirmed: Medium article "Continuation Setup," Warrior Trading course materials. "This prolonged momentum often results in a final short squeeze." — direct quote.
