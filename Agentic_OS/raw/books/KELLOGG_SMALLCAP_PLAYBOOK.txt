SETUP 1: LISTED MORNING PANIC DIP BUY
Classification: Scalp to intraday
Confidence: High
--------

Description:
A listed small-cap that gapped up or ran the prior day sells off hard and fast in
the first 30 minutes. Sellers overwhelm. Volume surges downward. Price crashes to
a structural level. The panic is mechanical and fast, not a slow grind lower.

What makes it work on listed names vs OTC:
On listed names there is no promoter-controlled soft landing. The bounce must be
driven by shorts covering, trapped buyers getting shaken out, or genuine support
at a prior structure level. This means the dip buy on a listed name requires a
harder structural confirmation before entry than an OTC dip buy.

Required context:
Stock must have a confirmed catalyst from the prior session or premarket.
Volume spike during the panic confirms exhaustion of sellers, not continuation.
VWAP must be above the entry point or the price must be recovering toward VWAP.
Clear stop exists at the panic low.

Entry trigger:
First stabilization candle after the panic. First green candle after the flush.
Not mid-panic. Wait for the momentum of the sell-off to visibly stall.

Stop: One to two ticks below the panic low. Tight.

Target:
Recovery to VWAP. Partial exit at VWAP.
If VWAP reclaimed with volume, hold partial for HOD test.
Do not hold through a re-test of the panic low.

Size: Start at 10-15% of intended position. Add only if VWAP reclaim holds.

Failure condition:
Panic low breached after entry = cut immediately. No averaging. No waiting.
"Panics don't always bounce. Cut losses and walk away."

Adaptation note from OTC version:
On OTCs, the promoter incentive created predictable soft landings.
On listed names, the soft landing must be justified by structural support
(prior day close, VWAP, prior breakout level, high-volume node).
If no structural level exists below the panic, the setup is not valid.

Classification: Scalp to short swing
Confidence: High
--------

Description:
Buying near the close of session when a listed small-cap is closing strong on a

A strong close near HOD on expanding volume on a named catalyst is a different

Required context:
Confirmed catalyst same day or after close.
Stock closing in top 10-20% of day's range.
End-of-day volume above average. Not thinning into close.
Bull market regime active. Bear regime = this setup stuffs at open.
Dilution review required. If active ATM/shelf confirmed = downgrade to scalp.

Entry trigger:
Buy within last 30 minutes of session if close confirms above support.
AH catalyst exception only with extreme catalyst quality (FDA, earnings beat confirmed).

Stop:
Prior day close. If stock opens below close = exit at open. No hold through gap-down.

Target:
Morning gap-up spike first 15 minutes. Trim 30-40% immediately on morning strength.
If stock consolidates elevated after the initial spike = hold partial with trail.
Hard exit: if stock fades immediately on open = 100% out within first 15 minutes.
Never hold an overnight listed play past 10:30 AM ET without continuation confirmation.

Dilution gate:
Active ATM, active shelf, or 424B filed within 60 days = remove from overnight
consideration. Overnight hold with active dilution = financing event into morning spike
is a real structural risk. The close-near-HOD signal does not override a live seller.

Size: Start at 50% of intended size. Add only if pre-market shows no adverse news.

SETUP 3: SHORT SQUEEZE ON LISTED THICK-FLOAT NAMES
Classification: Intraday to multi-day
Confidence: High
--------

Description:
A listed stock with a thick, well-defined float is being heavily shorted and a
catalyst or sustained buying forces short sellers to cover, accelerating the move.

Why Kellogg explicitly preferred thick-float listed names for large size:
"With the thick floats you have a very clear stop."
Float size creates predictable stop levels. The move is defined by the structural
mechanics of covering, not by thin-float volatility or halt risk.

Required context:
Confirmed short interest above 15-20% of float.
Catalyst that forces covering (earnings beat, FDA, sector momentum, short report failure).
Price holding above prior resistance level after initial squeeze move.
Volume expanding on each push higher, not contracting.
Not already extended 50%+ on day one without pullback.

Entry:
Do not buy at the beginning of the squeeze. Let the first push happen.
Entry on the first pullback that holds above VWAP after the initial spike.
Or entry on HOD break after consolidation if shorts failed to push price back down.

Stop:
Below the consolidation low after the squeeze begins. Below VWAP on reclaim play.

Target:
Prior HOD. Then measured move based on the base.
Trim aggressively into climax volume. Climax volume = the squeeze is ending, not
continuing. High volume at highs = potential exhaustion, not proof of more upside.
"Seeing big volume go through I know that potentially a lot of people are on the
wrong side."

Size:
His largest size was deployed on squeeze plays in listed thick-float names.
Thick float = clear stop = size can be larger without catastrophic slippage risk.
Liquidity check required before sizing large: can you exit 100% within two minutes?

Multi-day hold logic:
If sector theme is active and volume confirms each day, hold partial.
If sector cools or volume contraction appears = exit remaining position.
Do not hold squeeze beyond day 3-4 without fresh catalyst confirmation.

Failure condition:
T+2 settlement forces covering (T+1 buys, forced covers).
Once T+2 cycle clears, forced covering ends. Squeeze without new buyers = fade.
Exit before the forced cover exhaust period ends.

Prior significant resistance level.

Size:

SECTION 4 -- INDICATORS TRANSLATED TO SMALL-CAP LISTED CONTEXT
========================================

These are his confirmed four indicators, adapted specifically for listed small-cap use.

--------
INDICATOR 1: VWAP
--------

On listed names:
VWAP is more meaningful on listed stocks than OTCs because institutional order flow
participates. VWAP breaks and reclaims carry real signal weight.

Buy rule: Do not buy a listed small-cap long if price is materially above VWAP
without a clear reclaim setup. Buying above VWAP on a listed name means you are
buying above where the average participant entered that day. You are chasing.

Short awareness rule: Do not short a listed name below VWAP without confirmation.
VWAP below = bearish. Shorting below VWAP chases the move.

Profit-taking rule: VWAP is the first exit level on bounce trades.
If entry was below VWAP, first target is VWAP reclaim.

Stop-loss alignment: For dip buys, VWAP reclaim failure = exit signal.
If price recovers to VWAP but cannot hold, the bounce thesis is failing.


--------
INDICATOR 2: LINEAR REGRESSION CHANNEL
--------

On listed names:
Three-line overlay (upper band, midline, lower band).
Price above upper band = extended, potential reversal zone.
Price below lower band = oversold, potential bounce zone.
Used to confirm trend direction. Not used as a standalone entry signal.
"A stock moving well within a channel makes its price trend more predictable."
On listed small-caps: channel movement is cleaner in trending sectors, choppier
in non-trending names. Apply only when trend context is confirmed.


--------
INDICATOR 3: VOLUME
--------

On listed names:
Volume spike near HOD = potential exhaustion. Many people chasing. Reversal risk.
Volume spike near LOD after a panic = potential seller exhaustion. Bounce setup.
Volume expansion on breakout candle = confirms the move.
Volume contraction on pullback = healthy consolidation, continuation likely.
Churn (high volume, low net price movement) = hidden distribution. Exit immediately.
"Seeing big volume go through, I know that potentially a lot of people are on the
wrong side."
On listed names this is especially critical because institutional programs can create
high-volume absorption at tops that looks like buying but is distribution.


--------
INDICATOR 4: SUPPORT AND RESISTANCE
--------

On listed names:
Key levels defined by: price hovering for 30 minutes to one hour = strongest S/R.
Volume parallel to price at those levels confirms their significance.
Prior day high, prior day low, prior day close = structural reference levels.
Breakout price from the prior base = becomes support after successful breakout.
On listed small-caps, these levels carry more weight because institutional memory
holds them. OTC levels are often more fluid due to thin float.
