SECTION 1 -- SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

This system is built on one principle: catalyst-driven momentum with structural
confirmation beats pure technicals every time in small-cap swing trading. The
highest-probability swing trades occur when a real, verifiable catalyst forces
institutional-level volume into a float that cannot absorb it cleanly -- producing
multi-day continuation that is tradeable with a defined edge.

Four non-negotiables that define this entire system:

1. The catalyst must be graded before any technical work is done.
   Technical setup without catalyst grade is noise in small caps.

2. Dilution must be assessed relative to catalyst strength -- not blanket avoided.
   A grade-A catalyst can overcome moderate dilution risk. Grade-C cannot.

3. Volume quality -- not raw volume -- determines continuation probability.
   Expansion on pushes and contraction on pullbacks = distribution of supply upward.
   Churn (high volume, low net movement) = hidden supply. Exit immediately.

4. Position hold duration is determined by float, catalyst grade, and structure --
   not by a preferred timeframe. The setup picks the hold. Not the other way around.

---UNIVERSAL HARD FILTERS (applies to all tiers and all hold durations)---

1. Exchange: NYSE, NASDAQ, AMEX only. No OTC/Pink Sheet overnight holds.

4. Relative Volume (RVOL) at time of scan: Minimum 2.5x for overnight consideration.
   Minimum 4x for same-day entry on momentum plays.
   Below 2.5x = no institutional attention = no continuation fuel.

5. Reverse Split History: Any reverse split in the last 6 months = automatic skip.
   Two reverse splits in 12 months = hard permanent avoid. Never re-enters watchlist.

6. Float: See float-tiered criteria below.
   No hard upper cap on float for 5-10 day holds when catalyst is grade A.

7. Market Cap: $10M minimum to avoid OTC-level manipulation risk.
   $5B maximum for this system -- above $5B small-cap edge disappears.

8. No pending SEC trading suspension history in last 24 months.

9. No confirmed active PIPE or registered direct offering live and unresolved.
   If offering was announced within 60 days and shares not yet freely tradeable = skip.

Swing hold duration: Overnight only. Maximum 1-3 days if daily chart is pristine.
Target: Conservative. Half the measured move from base. Trail stops tight.

GRADE D -- No catalyst or negative catalyst misread as positive
Examples: Press release with no substance, vague LOI, third-party unverified claims,
          short squeeze suspected with no catalyst, volume spike with no news found.
Effect on dilution tolerance: Irrelevant -- Grade D names are not swing traded long.
Action: Monitor only. No entry. If strong technical breakout persists for 2+ days,
        re-evaluate and reassign grade if catalyst emerges.

SECTION 3 -- DUE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST (PRE-TRADE MANDATORY)
================================================================================

Run every step in order. Do not skip steps for time. If a step cannot be completed,
do not enter the trade until it is. Grade each item Pass / Flag / Fail.

---STEP 1: CATALYST VERIFICATION (10 minutes)---

1a. Find the original source of the catalyst. Stock Titan, SEC.gov, company IR page.
    Not a news aggregator -- the original filing or press release.
1b. Read the full text. Not the headline.
1c. Confirm: Is there a dollar amount, approval number, agency name, or quantifiable metric?
    Yes = catalyst has substance. No = downgrade catalyst grade by one level.
1d. Check if catalyst was already known 24+ hours ago (stale catalyst).
    Stale catalyst + price already moved 20%+ = entering late = Grade C at best.
1e. Assign Catalyst Grade: A / B / C / D using criteria from Section 2.

Pass criteria: Source confirmed. Catalyst substance confirmed. Grade assigned.
Fail criteria: Cannot find original source. Headline only. Stale by 24+ hours.

---STEP 2: DILUTION FORENSIC (15 minutes)---

2a. Pull SEC EDGAR: https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar
    Search ticker. Review last 6 months of filings.
2b. Look for the following filings (red flags):
    - S-3 or S-3ASR (shelf registration -- dilution on demand)
    - 424B filings (prospectus supplement -- active offering)
    - 8-K with "registered direct offering" or "PIPE" language
    - S-1 amendments (secondary offering incoming)
    - NT filings (missed reporting deadlines = financial stress)
2c. If any red flag found: cross-reference with catalyst grade.
    Grade A catalyst + S-3 only (no active offering): Watchable. Reduce size 30%.
    Grade A catalyst + active PIPE or 424B within 60 days: Skip.
    Grade B + any active offering: Size down 50%. Daily hard stop at -8%.
    Grade C + any red flag: Hard avoid.
2d. Check OS (Outstanding Shares) vs reported float. Large gap = warrant/convert overhang.
    OS > Float by 30%+ = hidden dilution. Treat as one level worse.
2e. Check recent reverse split history. See Section 2 hard filters.
2f. Assign Dilution Classification: Clean / Watch / Moderate / Toxic

Pass criteria: No active offering. S-3 allowed only if Grade A catalyst.
Fail criteria: Active PIPE or 424B filed within 60 days. Reverse split in 6 months.

---STEP 3: FLOAT QUALITY AND STRUCTURE (5 minutes)---

3a. Confirm float from Finviz or EDGAR reported shares minus institutional lockups.
3b. Calculate: Current day volume / reported float = float turnover ratio.
    Float turnover below 0.5x = insufficient retail interest for swing hold.
    Float turnover 1x-3x = ideal for 1-3 day hold.
    Float turnover above 5x in single session = over-extended on day 1 = wait for pullback.
3c. Check short interest % of float (Finviz, Nasdaq.com).
    Short interest above 20% of float with Grade A catalyst = squeeze fuel present.
    Short interest above 40% = potential squeeze, but also elevated borrow cost risk.
3d. Check institutional ownership. Above 40% in small-cap with fresh catalyst = real.
    Below 5% = entirely retail-driven = less reliable multi-day continuation.

Pass criteria: Float confirmed. Turnover ratio appropriate for hold duration.
Fail criteria: Float unclear or unavailable. Turnover above 5x on day 1 with no pullback plan.

---STEP 4: CHART STRUCTURE ANALYSIS (10 minutes)---

DAILY CHART FIRST:
4a. Identify the most recent base. A base is a consolidation of 5+ days with
    contracting volume and range. Flat base, cup, VCP, flag all qualify.
4b. Measure the base depth. Base depth should not exceed 30% from peak to trough.
    Over 30% = base too deep = buying at a weak point, not a pivot.
4c. Confirm the stock is breaking out of or near the top of the base. Not mid-base.
4d. Verify the 9EMA is above the 20EMA on the daily. This is the minimum trend stack.
    For 5-10 day holds: 9EMA > 20EMA > 50SMA preferred.
4e. Identify measured move target: base height added to breakout point.
    Measured move must offer minimum 15% upside from current price to justify entry.
4f. Mark all prior resistance levels: prior HOD, prior gap fill levels, warrant/offering prices.
    These become structural ceilings. Risk/reward must clear the nearest ceiling by 1R minimum.

60-MINUTE CHART:
4g. Confirm intraday trend direction matches daily trend. 60-min 9EMA above 20EMA.
4h. Look for consolidation on the 60-min chart. Tight range = coil = breakout fuel.
4i. VWAP position: price above prior day VWAP and current VWAP = structural strength.

15-MINUTE CHART (entry timing only):
4j. Use 15-min for entry trigger only. Do not base hold decision on 15-min.
4k. Entry trigger: 15-min candle close above the 60-min base high.
    Or: 15-min VWAP reclaim after clean pullback to VWAP.
    Or: 15-min red-to-green cross with volume expansion.

Pass criteria: Daily base confirmed. 9EMA > 20EMA. Measured move 15%+ available.
Fail criteria: No base identifiable. EMA stack inverted. Price mid-range with no pivot.

---STEP 5: VOLUME QUALITY CONFIRMATION (5 minutes)---

5a. Compare today's volume to 20-day average. Minimum 2.5x required.
5b. Look at volume distribution throughout the session:
    Expansion on green candles + contraction on red candles = buying pressure. Enter.
    Expansion on red candles + contraction on green = distribution. Do not enter.
    Same volume on red and green (churn) = uncertain. Wait for clarity.
5c. For overnight consideration: volume in the final 30 minutes of session should
    be above 20-day 30-minute average. Late volume = institutional accumulation.
    Selling into close = stock may gap down. Do not hold overnight.
5d. Review tape character if available:
    Bids stacking > asks stacking = buyers in control.
    Large prints above ask = aggressive buying.
    Large prints at bid refreshing every print = distribution. Avoid.

Pass criteria: RVOL confirmed. Volume expansion on pushes. Late-day volume positive.
Fail criteria: Churn dominant. Selling into close. Volume one-sided toward distribution.

---STEP 6: RISK/REWARD CALCULATION (3 minutes)---

6a. Define entry price. Use trigger point from Step 4j -- not current price.
6b. Define stop loss. Method: below the most recent structural support on the
    timeframe being traded. For daily swing: below the base low or day's low + buffer.
    For 60-min hold: below VWAP or 60-min base low.
    Stop distance: 3-8% for micro/low float. 5-12% for mid float.
    Never more than 12% stop on any swing trade. If structure requires 12%+ stop = no trade.
6c. Define targets:
    Target 1: 50% of measured move. Trim 30-40% of position here.
    Target 2: Full measured move. Trim additional 40% here.
    Target 3: Extended target if momentum and sector theme persist. Trail the remainder.
6d. Calculate R:R ratio. Entry to Stop = 1R. Entry to T1 must be minimum 1.5R.
    Entry to T2 must be minimum 2.5R. Do not enter if targets are below these thresholds.
6e. Assign position size tier:
    Grade A catalyst + Clean dilution: Full size (defined as your max position for account)
    Grade A + Watch dilution: 75% size
    Grade B + Clean: 75% size
    Grade B + Watch: 50% size
    Grade B + Moderate dilution: 25% size (scalp tier only)
    Grade C + Clean: 25% size (scalp tier -- overnight only)
    Any Grade C + any dilution flag: No trade.

Pass criteria: R:R 1.5:1 minimum to T1. 2.5:1 minimum to T2. Stop defined.
Fail criteria: Cannot define stop. R:R below 1.5:1 to any target. Targets blocked by ceiling.

SECTION 5 -- PLAYBOOK A: OVERNIGHT HOLD SETUP
================================================================================

Purpose: Enter EOD, hold through the night, exit next-morning gap or continuation.
Win rate optimization target: 65%+
Best conditions: Active catalyst, thin float, low supply overhead, sector momentum.

---ELIGIBLE SETUP TYPES FOR OVERNIGHT HOLDS---

1. EOD BREAKOUT HOLD
   Stock breaks HOD in final 45-60 minutes of session with expanding volume.
   Close at or near HOD (top 10% of day's range).
   Volume in final 30 min above 20-day 30-min average.
   No major resistance within 10% above closing price.
   Catalyst is Grade A or B. Dilution is Clean or Watch only.

2. VWAP RECLAIM LATE SESSION
   Stock pulled back to VWAP midday, absorbed supply cleanly (volume declining on pull).
   Reclaimed VWAP in afternoon session with expansion volume.
   Closing above VWAP with a higher low than the midday low.
   Same catalyst and dilution requirements as above.

3. TIGHT FLAG INTO CLOSE (Low Float / Micro Float Only)
   Stock made a big move early in session. Pulled back 30-50% of the move.
   Held above key level (VWAP or prior resistance turned support) for 3+ hours.
   Volume contracted during consolidation. Late-day squeeze forming.
   Grade A catalyst required for micro float. B acceptable for low float.

4. RED-TO-GREEN REVERSAL LATE SESSION
   Stock was red for majority of session but closed green or at breakeven.
   High-volume reversal candle in final 90 minutes.
   Closed above both the VWAP and prior day's close.
   Strongest setup type for the next-day gap up continuation.

---ENTRY RULES FOR OVERNIGHT---

Enter during the final 30 minutes of regular session (3:30-4:00 PM ET only).

As a percentage: No more than 6% stop for micro/low float. 8% max for mid float.
Trigger: If stock opens below the prior day's close gap-filled or below VWAP = exit immediately.
Do not wait for stop to be hit if open is bearish gap below your stop level.
Pre-market stop monitoring: Check pre-market by 8:00 AM ET minimum.
If pre-market shows negative sentiment change or dilution news = exit at open, not at stop.

---TARGET RULES FOR OVERNIGHT---

Morning spike target: Prior day high + 5-15% (depends on float and RVOL).
For micro floats: Plan for 20-50% morning spike if catalyst remains intact.
Scale out plan:
  Open + first 15 minutes pop: Trim 25-30% of position into morning strength.
  If stock holds and consolidates at elevated level: Trail remaining position.
  If stock fades immediately on open: Exit 100% within first 15 minutes. Do not hold.
Never hold an overnight position past 10:30 AM without a confirmed continuation trigger.

---OVERNIGHT RISK MANAGEMENT---

SECTION 6 -- PLAYBOOK B: 1-3 DAY HOLD SETUP
================================================================================

Purpose: Enter on confirmed breakout or pullback, hold for 2-3 days of continuation.
Win rate optimization target: 60-65%
Best conditions: Day 2 continuation after confirmed Day 1 catalyst. Sector tailwind.
Float preference: Low to mid float (5M-100M shares). Micro float tolerable if base is tight.

---ELIGIBLE SETUP TYPES FOR 1-3 DAY HOLDS---

1. DAY 2 CONTINUATION
   Stock had a confirmed catalyst + volume on Day 1.

   Entry: Day 2 breakout above Day 1 HOD with volume expansion.
   This is the single highest win rate setup in the 1-3 day category.

2. FIRST PULLBACK IN UPTREND
   Stock has been in an uptrend for 5+ days.

   Pulls back to the 9EMA or 20EMA on the daily.
   Entry: First green day off the EMA hold with volume expansion.
   Requires catalyst grade B+ and clean dilution only.

3. BASE BREAKOUT (daily chart)
   Stock consolidated for 5-15 days in a tight range (less than 15% wide).
   Breakout above range high on volume 2.5x+ above 20-day average.
   Entry: On daily close above base high or intraday on 15-min confirmation.
   Works best in sector momentum environments where the theme is active.

4. POST-EARNINGS MOMENTUM
   Earnings beat confirmed. EPS or revenue above estimates by 10%+.
   Stock gaps up 10-30% on earnings. Not a 50%+ gap -- those fade.
   Does not immediately fill the gap on Day 1.
   Entry: Day 2 or Day 3 after a tight consolidation of the gap area.
   Do not chase the gap-up candle on earnings day. Enter the base that forms after.

---ENTRY RULES FOR 1-3 DAY---

Entry window: Prefer entries in the first 90 minutes (9:30-11:00 AM ET).
Or: Final 60 minutes of session (3:00-4:00 PM ET) on EOD breakout confirmation.
Avoid midday entries (11:30 AM - 1:30 PM) -- liquidity drops, fakes increase.
Use multiple scale-in for larger positions:
  First entry: 50% of intended position at trigger confirmation.
  Second entry: 25% more if stock holds above trigger for 30 minutes.
  Third entry: Final 25% if stock breaks the next intraday resistance level.
  Do not average down. Only scale up into strength.

---STOP RULES FOR 1-3 DAY---

Initial stop: Below the trigger level (HOD of prior day, base breakout level, EMA).
Trail: After first 24 hours, trail stop to below the most recent higher low on 60-min chart.
After 48 hours: Trail stop to below prior day's low.
Use time-based stop rule: If no progress toward T1 after 3 full trading days = exit.
A sideways stock is a losing position in small caps -- the float turns over, supply builds.

---TARGET RULES FOR 1-3 DAY---

Target 1 (25-35% of position): Prior resistance level or measured move 50%.
Target 2 (35-40% of position): Full measured move from base.
Remaining (25-30%): Trail with 60-min close below 9EMA.
Hard exit no later than end of Day 3 unless setup has evolved into 5-10 day theme play.
If stock is up 30%+ in 2 days and sector is cooling: Do not hold the runner. Bank it.
If stock is up 50%+ in 2 days: Exit 80% immediately. Small cap 50% moves in 2 days fade.

SECTION 7 -- PLAYBOOK C: 5-10 DAY HOLD SETUP
================================================================================

Purpose: Hold a sector leader for a full multi-week move. Highest R:R potential.
Win rate optimization target: 55-60% (lower win rate, higher R:R per trade)
Best conditions: Confirmed sector rotation, institutional catalyst, clean float mid-cap.
Float preference: Low float (5-20M) or mid float (20-100M). Micro float too volatile for 5-10D.

The 5-10 day hold is a different psychological trade than the first two.
You must accept larger intraday swings and not micromanage.
The daily chart is the primary management tool. Intraday movement is noise.
Managing off 5-min charts during a 5-10 day hold = destroying the trade.

---ELIGIBLE SETUP TYPES FOR 5-10 DAY HOLDS---

1. VCP (VOLATILITY CONTRACTION PATTERN) BREAKOUT
   Stock has a base of 15-40 days with contracting price swings.
   Each pivot low in the base is shallower than the prior. Volume also declining.
   Final pivot (handle) is less than 8-10% deep.
   Breakout: Price closes above the right side of the pivot on 3x+ volume.
   This is the William O'Neil / Mark Minervini setup adapted for small caps.
   Most reliable for 5-10 day holds when catalyst grade is A.

2. STAGE 2 UPTREND PULLBACK
   Stock has been in a sustained uptrend for 20+ days.
   Pulls back for 4-7 days on declining volume to a key moving average (9 or 20 EMA).
   Holds the moving average. Forms a tight consolidation (3-5 days under 10% range).
   Re-entry: First daily close back above the 9 EMA with volume expansion.
   Requires sector tailwind and Grade A or B catalyst still active.

3. SECTOR LEADER IDENTIFICATION AND HOLD
   Identify the top-performing stock in an active sector theme (AI, biotech, defense, etc.)
   The leader is defined as: strongest 5-day return, highest RVOL sustained over 3 days,
   multiple analyst or institutional mentions in the same week.
   Enter the leader on a 1-3 day pullback to support.
   Hold as long as: daily close above 20-day SMA, sector ETF in uptrend, volume supportive.

4. POST-FDA APPROVAL CONTINUATION
   FDA approval announced (Grade A catalyst -- highest tier).
   Stock gaps up. Day 1-2 is volatile and distribution from early holders.
   Wait for 3-5 day consolidation of the gap to form a base.
   Enter the break above the gap consolidation high with tight stop at consolidation low.
   These setups can run 40-100% over 5-10 days on true approval catalysts.

---DAILY MANAGEMENT RULES FOR 5-10 DAY HOLDS---

Review charts once -- at 4:30 PM ET after close. Do not manage intraday.
Exception: Pre-market dilution news, sector ETF -5% or more, halt for investigation.
Primary stop: Daily close below the 20-day SMA = exit 50%.
             Two consecutive daily closes below 20-day SMA = exit remaining 50%.
Do not exit on intraday dips to the 20-day SMA if the daily candle recovers and closes above.
Trail stop rule: Each new higher low on the daily chart = trail stop to 1% below that low.

---TARGET LADDER FOR 5-10 DAY HOLDS---

Target 1 (20% of position): First resistance level above entry -- prior high or prior gap.
Target 2 (30% of position): Measured move from base.
Target 3 (30% of position): 1.5x measured move. Trail with 20-day SMA.
Target 4 (20% runner): Let this run as long as: daily above 20 SMA, sector in trend.
Hard close: Day 10 is the maximum unless the stock has made a higher-low structure above
            the 20-day SMA and the move is still clearly intact.

---SECTOR THEME PROTOCOL FOR 5-10 DAY---

Define the sector theme explicitly before holding beyond Day 5.
Example themes: "Small cap defense post-contract" / "Biotech post-FDA" / "AI hardware cycle"
Monitor the sector ETF daily. If the ETF fails below its 20-day SMA = close all 5-10D holds.
Find 2-3 names in the same theme. Spread risk across the theme leaders.
If one name breaks down but sector is intact = it was a laggard. Hold the leaders.
If the sector itself breaks = exit all theme positions regardless of individual stock charts.

SECTION 9 -- POSITION SIZING FRAMEWORK (BUILDING FROM FLEXIBLE)
================================================================================

This framework builds a structured risk model from scratch. Adjust to your account size.

---BASE UNIT DEFINITION---

Step 1: Define your maximum acceptable loss per trade in dollar terms.
        Recommended: 1-2% of total trading capital per swing trade.
        Example: $30,000 account = $300-$600 max loss per trade.
        This dollar amount = 1R (one unit of risk).

Step 2: Define position size from stop distance.
        Formula: Position Size = Max Loss ($) divided by Stop Distance ($)
        Example: Max loss $400. Stop distance $0.80. Position size = 500 shares.
        If position size requires more than 25% of account in one name = reduce max loss.

Step 3: Apply the size tier from Step 6f of the DD Checklist:
        Full size = 100% of formula position size.
        75% size = 75% of formula position size. 50% = half. 25% = quarter.

Step 4: Never exceed these portfolio concentration limits:
        Single position: Max 20% of total account value.
        Single sector: Max 40% of total account value.
        Total overnight exposure: Max 50% of total account value.
        Total exposure at any time: Max 80% of total account value (20% cash buffer minimum).

---TIERED ACCOUNT THRESHOLDS---

Under $10K account:
  Maximum 1 active swing position at a time.
  Use 1% risk per trade ($100 max loss on $10K).
  Only Grade A catalysts. Only Clean dilution. No exceptions.
  Micro float only for overnight. No 5-10 day holds.

$10K-$50K account:
  Maximum 2-3 active swing positions.
  Use 1-1.5% risk per trade.
  All setups available. All hold durations available.
  No more than 2 positions in the same sector.

$50K-$200K account:
  Maximum 4-5 active swing positions.
  Use 0.75-1.25% risk per trade.
  All setups available.
  Require minimum 3 of 6 DD steps confirmed before sizing above 50%.

Above $200K account:
  Maximum 6-8 active swing positions.
  Use 0.5-1% risk per trade.
  Liquidity becomes a constraint on micro-float names.
  Micro float (<5M): Position size must be under 5% of average daily dollar volume.
  Respect liquidity constraints -- size down or skip micro floats at this account level.

# GRANT SWING SYSTEM -- SCANNER 2: 1-3 DAY SWING SCAN
# Identifies day 2 continuation, base breakouts, and first pullback entries
# Run daily at open and after close

input MinPrice2 = 1.00;
input MaxPrice2 = 50.00;
input BreakoutRVOL = 2.5;
input PullbackRVOL = 1.5;
input LookbackBars2 = 20;

def rvol2 = volume / avgVol2;

# EMA trend stack
def ema9_2 = ExpAverage(close, 9);
def ema20_2 = ExpAverage(close, 20);
def sma50 = Average(close, 50);
def trendStack = ema9_2 > ema20_2 and ema20_2 > sma50;

# Base breakout: today's close above highest high of prior 10 bars
def priorHighBreak = close > Highest(high[1], 10);

# Day 2 continuation: yesterday was a strong green day, today opens flat or higher
def priorDayStrong = (close[1] - low[1]) / (high[1] - low[1]) >= 0.65;
def todayNotFading = close >= close[1] * 0.97;

# First pullback entry: 3-5 bar pullback ending at 9EMA touch
def pullbackToEMA = low <= ema9_2 * 1.02 and close > ema9_2 and close[1] < ema9_2[1];

# Volume conditions
def breakoutVolume = rvol2 >= BreakoutRVOL;
def pullbackVolContraction = volume < avgVol2 * 1.0 and rvol2[1] < 1.0 and rvol2[2] < 1.0;

# Price range filter
def priceRange2 = close >= MinPrice2 and close <= MaxPrice2;

# Minimum volume filter
def minVol2 = volume >= 200000;

# Final signal: breakout OR day 2 continuation OR first pullback
def breakoutSignal = priorHighBreak and breakoutVolume and trendStack;
def day2Signal = priorDayStrong and todayNotFading and rvol2 >= 2.0 and trendStack;
def pullbackSignal = pullbackToEMA and pullbackVolContraction and trendStack;

plot Swing1to3Signal = priceRange2
    and minVol2
    and (breakoutSignal or day2Signal or pullbackSignal);

SECTION 13 -- FAILURE MODE MAP (WHAT KILLS THE TRADE)
================================================================================

Know these before you enter. If any trigger, the action is listed.

FAILURE MODE 1 -- FALSE BREAKOUT
Signs: Price breaks HOD or base high, immediately fades back below within 2 candles.
Action: If 15-min candle closes back below breakout level = exit 100%. No averaging.
Prevention: Wait for a full 15-min candle close above breakout before entering.

FAILURE MODE 2 -- DILUTION SURPRISE
Signs: 8-K filed with new offering. Price gaps down pre-market. Ask floods at open.
Action: Exit pre-market at market open. Do not wait for stop to be hit.
Prevention: DD Step 2 (EDGAR check) before every single entry.

FAILURE MODE 3 -- SUPPLY CEILING HIT
Signs: Price reaches a known warrant strike, prior offering price, or key resistance level.
       Ask refreshes consistently at that level. Price cannot close above for 2+ candles.
Action: Exit 50% immediately. Tight trail on remainder to 0.5% below current low.
Prevention: Map all ceiling levels in DD Step 4. Never ignore a known ceiling.

FAILURE MODE 4 -- SECTOR ROTATION OUT
Signs: Sector ETF drops below 20-day SMA while individual stock is still elevated.
Action: Accelerate exits. Do not wait for technical breakdown on the individual chart.
Prevention: Check sector ETF daily as part of position management.

FAILURE MODE 5 -- CHURN VOLUME
Signs: High volume but price barely moves. Large candle wicks both directions.
       Tape shows alternating large bids and asks at same level.
Action: Exit 100%. Churn = hidden distribution. This stock is being sold, not bought.
Prevention: Volume quality check (DD Step 5) before entry.

FAILURE MODE 6 -- TIME STOP
Signs: Position is flat or slightly down after 2x the expected hold duration.
Action: Exit 100%. A stock that doesn't move in the expected time is using up capital.
Prevention: Set time stops at the start of every swing: overnight by 10:30 AM.
            1-3 day by end of Day 3. 5-10 day by end of Day 10.

FAILURE MODE 7 -- HALT RISK (Micro Float)
Signs: Stock halted intraday. Unknown reason (not circuit breaker pattern -- that is predictable).
Action: Wait for reopen. Do not enter new orders during the halt.
        If halted for SEC investigation or trading suspension = this is an exit, not a hold.
Prevention: Avoid OTC names. Avoid stocks with pending SEC correspondence.
            Check SEC.gov for any open investigation filings before entry on micro floats.
