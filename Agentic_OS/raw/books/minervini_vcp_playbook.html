Section 01 · Playbook Overview & Core Operating Logic Mark Minervini · SEPA / VCP System

PLAYBOOK OVERVIEW

Mark Minervini is a techno-fundamentalist: he screens for exceptional earnings growth first, then uses technical chart analysis to time the entry at the lowest possible risk. His complete system is called SEPA — Specific Entry Point Analysis.

The core insight is that institutional investors drive big moves, and their activity leaves footprints in price and volume. The VCP pattern is how those footprints appear before a breakout. Minervini's job is to read those footprints and enter just before the majority of the move happens.

Core Principle: Align three forces simultaneously — strong fundamentals, constructive price action (Stage 2 + VCP), and a healthy overall market — and only then enter at the pivot. Without all three, do not trade.

The Operating Logic in 5 Steps

  1. Screen the universe. Use the 8-point Trend Template to eliminate 95%+ of stocks. Only Stage 2 uptrends qualify.
  2. Verify the fundamentals. Confirm accelerating EPS growth, rising revenues, expanding margins, and RS rating of 70+.
  3. Identify the VCP. Look for progressively tighter price contractions with drying volume — 2 to 6 contractions narrowing to a final tight pivot.
  4. Enter the pivot breakout. Buy one cent above the pivot resistance on expanding volume. Enter as close to the stop as possible to maximize position size for the same dollar risk.
  5. Manage with strict rules. Hard stop at 7–8% max. Take first partials at 2–3× risk. Trail with 10-day MA on remaining shares. Never average down.

Core Philosophy

  • STAGE 2 Stocks only make their biggest moves in Stage 2. Buying in Stage 1, 3, or 4 is provably inferior.
  • VCP PATTERN Contracting volatility = supply drying up. When supply stops, any demand sends price sharply higher.
  • TIGHT ENTRY Entering near the stop means a large position can be sized with the same dollar risk. Risk-first math.

What Minervini Trades

Section 03 · The VCP — Volatility Contraction Pattern (Core Setup) Mark Minervini · SEPA / VCP System

THE VCP — VOLATILITY CONTRACTION PATTERN

The VCP is the centerpiece of Minervini's entry timing system. It is not a simple chart shape — it is a behavioral sequence that reveals institutional accumulation in progress.

SETUP 1: THE STANDARD VCP
Core Setup · Swing Trade · Daily Chart Primary

What It Is

A stock in a Stage 2 uptrend pauses and consolidates. During this consolidation, each successive price pullback gets smaller and each period of contraction shows lower volume than the previous one. This tightening sequence — typically 2 to 6 contractions — is the VCP. The final contraction narrows into a specific price level called the pivot point.

When the stock breaks above the pivot with a surge in volume, it signals that supply has been absorbed and institutional buyers are in control.

Minervini's description: "When you quiet the stock down and it gets very tight on that right side after contracting and the volume comes in, that's telling you that stock supply has stopped coming to market. That's why they're so explosive when they come out of these formations."

Why It Works

  • Retail and impatient holders sell during the pullbacks — supply dries up
  • Institutions accumulate quietly on the quieter pullbacks
  • Each tighter contraction proves fewer sellers remain
  • When supply fully exhausts, even modest demand sends price sharply higher
  • The pattern exists in stocks with genuine earnings acceleration — not just chart games

VCP Anatomy — The Contraction Sequence

EXAMPLE CONTRACTION SEQUENCE

Contraction 1 ~20–30% pullback · Heavy volume
Contraction 2 ~10–15% pullback · Lighter volume
Contraction 3 ~5–8% pullback · Low volume
★ Pivot Area Very tight range · Volume drying · ENTRY ZONE

What Must Be True

  • Each contraction is smaller in % than the prior one
  • Volume is lower on each successive pullback (volume contraction)
  • Stock holds higher lows — no lower lows through the base
  • "Tennis ball" bounces off pullbacks — quick snappy recoveries
  • Closes near the upper end of daily ranges during the tight phase
  • A clear, identifiable pivot point (resistance level) visible on the chart
Required Context
All 8 Trend Template criteria confirmed. Stage 2 uptrend. Fundamental screen passed. Prior strong advance in the base. Overall market in a healthy bull phase.
Entry Trigger
One cent above the pivot high on a day with volume 40–50%+ above the 50-day average volume. Volume expansion on the breakout is mandatory — no volume, no entry.
Stop Logic
Below the low of the last (tightest) contraction. Maximum 7–8% below entry. If the correct stop would be wider than 8%, skip the trade entirely — the setup is not tight enough yet.
Target / Exit Logic
First partial sell at 2–3× initial risk (if risk is 5%, first target is 10–15% gain). Move stop to breakeven. Trail remaining shares with 10-day MA. Exit if stock closes below 10-day MA on heavy volume.
Common Failure Mode
Breakout on light volume that immediately reverses ("squat"). Stock breaks pivot but sellers rush in. If it gives back the pivot and closes below it, exit. Do not hold and hope.
Confidence Level
HIGH — Named, described, and demonstrated across both books, multiple interviews, live trade examples, and the Master Trader Program curriculum.

How to Identify a VCP in Real Time

  • ✓Stock passes all 8 Trend Template criteria
  • ✓Prior advance of at least 25–30% before the base formed
  • ✓At least 2 visible price contractions, each smaller than the last
  • ✓Volume lower on each successive pullback than the prior one
  • ✓No new lows being made — higher lows throughout the base
  • ✓Currently in the tightest part of the pattern (near the right side)
  • ✓A clear price level acts as resistance (the pivot)
  • ✓Volume currently near its lowest point in the base (the quiet before breakout)
  • ✗Do NOT enter if contractions are widening, not narrowing
  • ✗Do NOT enter if volume is rising during the pullback phases
  • ✗Do NOT enter before the breakout occurs — wait for pivot confirmation

Section 04 · Extended Setup Catalog — Power Play, Low Cheat & Squat Mark Minervini · SEPA / VCP System

SETUP CATALOG

Beyond the standard VCP, Minervini documents several specific setup variants. All operate on the same supply-exhaustion logic but with different structural conditions.

SETUP 2: THE POWER PLAY (HIGH TIGHT FLAG)
Rarer · Explosive · High-Velocity Moves

What It Is

A stock that has already surged 100%+ in 8 weeks or fewer and then consolidates in a tight base of 10–25% depth over 3–6 weeks. The Power Play is essentially a VCP forming on an already-explosive move rather than a standard advance.

This is the rarest setup but historically produces some of the fastest and largest subsequent moves. The prior velocity of the initial surge is the key distinguishing factor.

Why it works: A stock that has already moved 100%+ in under 8 weeks has demonstrated extraordinary demand. The consolidation that follows is typically brief and shallow because sellers who wanted to exit already did so during the move.

Precise Criteria (Confirmed from Books)

  • Prior move: 100%+ in 8 weeks or fewer
  • Consolidation depth: no more than 20–25%
  • Duration: 3–6 weeks (some as short as 12 days)
  • VCP characteristics must be present within the flag
  • Trend Template criteria still pass
  • Volume dry-up during the flag is essential

Historical Example (Confirmed)

TASR (now AXON) formed three separate Power Play / High Tight Flag setups within a 2,500% run over 9 months in 2003 — one of the most cited examples in Minervini's teaching materials.

Entry
Pivot breakout above the high of the tight consolidation, on volume 40%+ above average.
Stop
Below the low of the tight flag / consolidation. With Power Plays, stop can be tighter than normal VCPs due to the precision of the formation.
Target
First target is the prior high or a measured move equal to the prior flag structure. These can extend significantly further — hold with trailing stop.
Confidence

SETUP 3: THE LOW CHEAT
Early Entry · Lower Risk · Stage 2 Only

An alternative entry used when a stock is still within the Trend Template but is currently trading below the 50-day MA, pulling back within an otherwise healthy Stage 2 uptrend.

This is Minervini's "exception" entry — buying a pullback within a confirmed Stage 2 advance rather than waiting for the full pivot breakout. It is noted as the only exception to Rule 8 of the Trend Template (price above 50-day MA).

  • Stock must still pass criteria 1–7 of the Trend Template
  • The pullback to or below the 50-day must be on declining volume
  • Only valid within a strong, confirmed Stage 2 uptrend
  • Stop goes below the 50-day MA or the recent pullback low
  • Entry is lower risk because you're closer to the stop than at the breakout
MEDIUM — Mentioned in books and teaching but less frequently demonstrated publicly than standard VCP. Use with caution until pattern recognition skill is high.
SETUP 4: SQUAT REVERSAL
Post-Breakout · Re-Entry · Defined Window

After a legitimate breakout from a VCP, the stock retreats back into its prior base area — the "squat." This is a potential failure signal, but Minervini notes it can also be a re-entry opportunity if specific conditions are met.

When to hold through a squat: Volume subsides, price action tightens, and the stock stays above its stop. In a bull market, a squat that holds and recovers within 10 days can resume the uptrend.

When to exit immediately: Volume rises during the squat, price breaks through the 20-day MA on heavy volume, or the thesis fundamentally changes.

  • Real example: SCHW (October 2021, Minervini's 334% U.S. Investing Championship year)
  • Entered on gap-up after a squat reversal held at the right level
HIGH — Confirmed in book, live trade examples, and TraderLion documented recap of 2021 championship trades.
SETUP 5: IPO BASE / FIRST PRIMARY BASE
Highest Potential · Timing Critical

Minervini explicitly states that every bull market is characterized by a few leadership IPO stocks. The first base a new IPO forms after coming public is called the "primary base" — and it often produces the stock's largest subsequent advance.

The key rule: the IPO must be at least 2 months old before it is considered. This allows a proper base to form and provides enough price history to assess the trend structure.

  • IPO must have proven itself in the market for 2+ months
  • A primary base must form with VCP characteristics
  • Volume action must confirm institutional accumulation
  • Fundamentals must be exceptional — IPOs without growth are avoided
  • Entry is on the pivot breakout above the base, same as standard VCP

Section 05 · Execution Framework — Entry, Sizing, Scaling, and Exit Logic Mark Minervini · SEPA / VCP System

EXECUTION FRAMEWORK

Execution is where the edge is protected or destroyed. Minervini's system has specific rules for how to enter, how to size, how to add, and how to exit — each designed to keep the risk-reward math in his favor.

Entry Mechanics

The Specific Entry Point

  • Enter one cent above the pivot high — not before, not well above
  • Volume must be expanding intraday on the breakout day
  • Entry should be as close to the stop as possible (tight stop = larger position for same dollar risk)
  • Minervini uses limit orders, not market orders
  • The entry logic is risk-first: know the stop price before you know the size
HIGH

Position Sizing — The Risk-First Formula

THE FORMULA

Max $ Risk per Trade ÷ Stop Distance % = Position Size %

Example: If max risk = 1% of portfolio and stop = 8% below entry → Position size = 12.5% of portfolio

Example: If max risk = 1% and stop = 4% → Position size = 25% of portfolio

Tighter stops → Larger positions → Same dollar risk. This is why precise entries matter.

Adding to Positions

Rule: Add only to winning positions that are already working. Never add to a position that is flat or down. The quote is direct: "There is no intelligent reason to increase your trading size if your positions are showing losses."
  • Pyramid into strength — add as the stock proves itself
  • Add in smaller increments than the original position
  • Each add must have a defined stop and still maintain the R:R target
  • Do not add so much that a reversal gives back most of the unrealized gain

Trimming / Profit-Taking Strategy

Scaling Out Protocol (Confirmed)

  1. Take a partial sale when gain = 2–3× your initial stop (e.g., risk was 7%, sell part at +14–21%)
  2. Move stop to breakeven after the first partial sale
  3. Continue to hold remaining shares and trail the stop
  4. Trail with the 10-day MA — exit on a close below it on heavy volume
  5. For parabolic vertical moves: take profits aggressively during the vertical spike
  6. In weak markets: take profits quicker and at smaller gains
HIGH

Stop Loss Rules

Non-Negotiable Stop Rules

  • Maximum stop: 7–8% below entry (hard limit, never exceed)
  • Typical stop: 5–6% in normal conditions; tighter in good markets
  • In weak markets: tighten to 4–5% and take quicker profits
  • Exit without question when stop is hit — no emotional override
  • If a stock moves 10–15% against entry, the entry timing was wrong
  • Stop is placed at the logical technical level (below the contraction low), not at an arbitrary percentage
Minervini's discovery (from his own account analysis): He calculated that normalizing a 15% loss to a 10% stop would have turned a losing year into a 72% gain year. Tighter stops — even with more frequent small losses — dramatically improved overall performance.

Portfolio Concentration

  • Optimal: 4–6 stocks for most accounts
  • Maximum: 10–12 stocks for large portfolios
  • True 2:1 trader → optimal position size ≈ 25% per position (4 stocks equally weighted)
  • Never exceed 20 positions (5% weight each minimum)
  • Over-diversification dilutes gains and prevents focus on best setups

Signs of a Low-Quality Trade or Setup — Do Not Enter

Trend Template Failures

  • Any of the 8 criteria fails
  • 200-day MA declining or flat
  • Moving averages not stacked (50 < 150 or 50 < 200)
  • RS rating below 70
  • Price more than 25% below 52-week high

VCP Pattern Failures

  • Pullbacks widening instead of contracting
  • Heavy volume on the red pullback days
  • Lower lows forming in the base
  • No clear pivot/resistance level visible
  • Breakout on below-average volume

Behavioral / Execution Failures

  • Chasing a breakout that is already extended
  • Entering before the pivot is confirmed
  • Averaging down into a losing position
  • Holding through a stop because "it will come back"
  • Trading aggressively in a hostile market
Section 06 · Risk Management Model Mark Minervini · SEPA / VCP System

RISK MANAGEMENT

Risk management is the structural backbone of SEPA. It is not a reactive afterthought — it is built into the entry architecture itself. Every trade starts with the stop. Everything else is calculated from there.

The Two Core Risk Rules

1

Maximum Loss Per Trade: 7–8%

This is the hard ceiling. The stop is placed at the logical technical level first; if that level is more than 8% away, the trade is not taken. Not a guideline — a rule.

2

Winners Must Be Larger Than Losers

Minervini operates at a 45–60% win rate. The math only works because average winners (20–30%+) are 3–4× the average losers (5–7%). Asymmetric outcomes are the foundation of the strategy.

The Math of Survival

Geometric loss math (Minervini's own teaching): A 20% loss requires a 25% gain to recover. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain. "Once you start going over 10%, the math starts working against you."

Win Rate Reality Check

ScenarioResult
5 winners at avg +25%, 5 losers at avg -7.5%+87.5% net on deployed capital
One 100% winner + twelve 7–8% losersStill net positive
Win rate drops to 40%Still profitable if R:R ratio maintained

Progressive Exposure & Market-Conditional Sizing

Normal Conditions

  • Position size calculated backward from max loss tolerance
  • Typical stop: 5–6% (tight entries enabled by VCP precision)
  • Concentrated portfolio of 4–6 positions
  • Add to winners in smaller increments once position proves itself

Weak / Hostile Market — Trader Rehab Mode

When drawdowns accumulate or market conditions deteriorate, Minervini scales down systematically. This is not optional — it is part of the written system.

  • Reduce position size (e.g., 5,000 shares → 1,000 shares)
  • Take profits quicker than normal (10–12% instead of 20–25%)
  • Tighten stop to 4–5% instead of 7–8%
  • Reduce total portfolio exposure — move to cash
  • Focus only on the cleanest, highest-quality VCP setups
  • Do not trade bigger to recover losses. Recover accuracy first.
HIGH

Profit Protection Rules

  • Once a trade gains 2–3× the initial risk, yesterday's profit is today's principal
  • Move stop to breakeven after first partial sale
  • Never let a position that was once up 20%+ turn into a loss
  • In strong moves: trail the 10-day MA aggressively
  • In parabolic vertical moves: sell into the vertical spike — do not wait for the top

Section 07 · Psychology, Process Rules & Recurring Mental Models Mark Minervini · SEPA / VCP System

PSYCHOLOGY & PROCESS

Minervini's psychology framework is not motivational filler — it is the operating system that keeps the mechanical rules functional under real-money pressure.

Core Mental Models

1
Lose correctly before winning big.

"It's better to lose correctly than to win incorrectly." Taking the correct small loss teaches discipline. A large loss from ignoring the stop teaches the wrong lesson even if the stock eventually recovers.

2
Ego is your enemy.

Traders have to accept being wrong 40–55% of the time. Ego resistance to taking losses is the single most destructive force in the system. The market is always right; only the trader can be wrong.

3
Compound the capital, not the mistakes.

"To compound your money, and not your mistakes, your goal is to buy on the way up — not on the way down." Averaging down compounds the mistake. Averaging up compounds the win.

4
Specialize ruthlessly.

"Specialists get paid well, while those who know a little about many things make good conversation at parties." The VCP/SEPA methodology only works if it is practiced with enough repetition to develop genuine pattern recognition.

5
Process over outcomes, short term.

One trade does not define the system. The system is evaluated over a statistically significant sample of trades — at minimum, 20–30 trades before drawing conclusions about what is working or not.

Discipline Rules

  • ✓Run the Trend Template screen before generating a watchlist
  • ✓Confirm fundamentals before chart analysis
  • ✓Identify the VCP structure and pivot before the market opens
  • ✓Enter on the pivot breakout only — not before the pivot
  • ✓Define the stop price before the entry is placed
  • ✓Take partial profits systematically at 2–3× risk
  • ✓Scale down during losing streaks — do not press harder
  • ✓Review trade statistics regularly and eliminate underperforming categories
  • ✗Never average down into a losing position
  • ✗Never hold through a stop loss out of hope
  • ✗Never trade aggressively in a hostile market environment
  • ✗Never jump strategy mid-drawdown before mastering the current one
  • ✗Never enter a breakout that is already well-extended past the pivot

Daily Process (Reconstructed)

  1. Pre-market: Screen universe through Trend Template. Narrow to stocks with VCP structures forming or near pivot.
  2. Fundamental check: Verify earnings acceleration and RS for top candidates.
  3. Chart review: Mark pivot levels and set alerts for breakouts.
  4. Market open: Monitor volume on breakouts. Enter only confirmed pivots with expanding volume.
  5. Intraday: Monitor existing positions. Apply stop rules without hesitation if triggered.
  6. Post-market: Journal all trades. Track win rate, average gain, average loss, R:R. Identify which setups or sectors are underperforming.
  7. Weekly: Review statistics. Cut underperforming categories. Increase focus on best-performing patterns.

4 Layers to Add
