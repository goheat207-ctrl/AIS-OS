Section 01 · Playbook Overview & Core Foundation Roland Wolf · Wolf Trades

Playbook Overview

Roland Wolf's strategy is built on a single contrarian premise: most retail traders lose because they chase breakouts and buy strength. His edge is doing the opposite — buying structured weakness before the breakout happens, targeting the moment that short sellers are trapped and buyers re-establish control.

The method is primarily long-biased and applied to small-cap momentum stocks. It does not rely on indicators. It relies on understanding who is trapped, where supply is exhausted, and when institutional float rotation creates forced buying pressure.

Core Principle: Study how short-sellers think. Then position as the counterparty at exactly the moment they are most vulnerable to a squeeze.

The Strategy in 4 Rules

  • Identify a small-cap stock with a news catalyst and momentum.
  • Wait for the pullback — do not buy breakouts or high-of-day.
  • Enter at or near a defined support level where sellers are exhausted.
  • Sell into returning strength with a minimum 2:1 R:R.

This System Is Built On

  • Short-seller psychology and trap mechanics
  • Float rotation and T+1 settlement dynamics
  • Data tracking per ticker to recognize repeatable behavior
  • Patience: waiting for the setup, not forcing trades
  • Anti-complexity: no indicators, no complicated setups

Core Foundation

The system is built around one core truth:

Markets are arenas where short-sellers create the fuel that long buyers ignite.

  • Support = Where short sellers are over-extended and buyers absorb supply
  • Resistance = Where sellers gain temporary control and shorts press aggressively
  • Catalyst = Required fuel for price to overcome seller resistance
  • Float = The constraint that amplifies all directional moves

Timeframes Used

  • Primary: Daily chart for structure and context
  • Intraday: 5-minute chart for timing entries
  • Focus Window: Market open through ~11:00 AM EST
  • Swing Trades: Same logic applied on daily timeframe

SETUP 1: The Structured Dip Buy
Core Setup · Long

Concept

This is Roland's core setup and the foundation of everything else. After a stock runs hard on catalyst, it pulls back to a defined support level. Roland enters on that dip before the breakout attempt, not after the stock confirms the high.

The goal is to capture the move from dip to prior high (or new high) with risk defined below the pullback low. This offers a favorable R:R while entering where most retail traders are afraid.

Key Insight: "I don't want to buy into a breakout. I want to buy long before the breakout even happens." — Roland Wolf (Tim Sykes Blog, direct quote, confirmed)

Required Context

  • Stock has a real catalyst (news, SEC filing)
  • Low float or crowded short positioning
  • Hot or trending market environment
  • Former runner with prior large-percent move
  • Volume above average on the initial run

Common Failure Mode

  • Support fails → stock continues lower (stop out)
  • Low-volume pullback with no bounce confirmation
  • Broad market selloff negates individual catalyst
  • Entering too early before selling exhausts itself
Entry Trigger
Stock pulls back to defined support (prior day high, whole number, VWAP). Enter when selling slows and volume dries up on the dip — not at breakout of prior high.
Stop Logic
Below the most recent swing low / support level. Must be a defined price level, not arbitrary. If support fails, thesis is invalidated.
Target Logic
Prior high or next major resistance level. Minimum 2:1 R:R required. Scale partial at first resistance; leave runners for extension toward prior high of day.
Confidence
High — Confirmed repeatedly in direct statements, Tim Sykes blog, Humbled Traders podcast, IU interview topics.
SETUP 2: The Dip & Rip (Opening Flush + Reclaim)
Intraday · Long · High Urgency

Concept

A stock gaps up on news, sells off hard at the open (shaking out weak longs and trapping early shorts), then "rips" back and reclaims the pre-market highs. Roland explicitly covers this pattern in the Investors Underground interview.

The key is that the initial sell-off creates the trap. Shorts who pressed into the flush are now wrong when price rips back up. Their forced covers add momentum to Roland's long position.

Setup Rules

  • Stock gaps up pre-market on real news
  • Opening flush — sells off hard early (weak hands shaken out)
  • Look for bounce off support with volume surge
  • Price must reclaim pre-market highs to confirm
  • Enter on the reclaim or the pullback into the reclaim
  • Target: high of day extension / prior resistance
Confirmed — IU interview topics: "Dip & Rip: How Traps Get Set"
Entry Trigger
Volume surge off flush low + candle closes back above pre-market support or whole number. Do NOT enter during the flush — wait for exhaustion signal.
Invalidation
Flush continues below all major support with no volume absorption. Catalyst proven fake or stock in confirmed downtrend. No buyers step in.
Why This Works
Short sellers who pressed the morning flush are trapped. As price reclaims highs, they cover — adding buying pressure on top of organic demand.
Confidence
High — Directly named and discussed in Investors Underground interview. Confirmed setup in Wolf's catalog.
SETUP 3: Short Squeeze Long
Advanced · Float-Dependent

Concept

Roland identifies stocks with high short interest as a percent of float where a rally will trigger forced covers. He enters on the dip/support, expecting shorts to fuel the next leg up through mechanical covering. He studies what triggers a squeeze, not just that one might happen.

Critically, he tracks T+1 settlement mechanics — knowing that failure-to-deliver windows can create additional forced buying pressure on specific days after a spike.

Key Components

  • High short interest % float (crowded short thesis)
  • Price near support where shorts are at risk
  • T+1 or T+2 window for forced cover pressure
  • Float rotation creates repeat squeeze opportunities
  • "When the squeeze ends" — Roland also understands when NOT to be long (fade the backside)
Confirmed — IU Interview: "What Triggers a Short Squeeze," "T+1 Buys & Forced Covers," "Float Rotation Explained," "When the Squeeze Ends: Fading the Backside"
Entry Trigger
Stock holds support with shorts over-positioned. Enter on dip into support when volume signals buyers stepping in. NOT on the initial squeeze rip.
Exit Logic
Scale out as short covering pressure accelerates. Full exit when squeeze mechanics complete (backside of move = danger zone). Switch to watching for fade setup.
Risk Factor
Crowded trades carry black swan risk — if too many longs pile in, any bad news creates avalanche selling. Roland explicitly warns against this crowded-trade risk.
Confidence
High — Named in IU interview, discussed in depth. Core to his understanding of small-cap mechanics.

Execution Framework

Roland's execution philosophy is defined by one concept: patience until the setup, then decisive action. He does not trade every day. He does not force entries. When the setup presents itself, he acts without hesitation.

Entry Style

Anti-breakout by design. He explicitly avoids buying whole-dollar breakouts or high-of-day spikes. Entries are taken on controlled pullbacks to defined support — where most retail traders are scared.

Confirmed Principle: "90% or more of retail traders lose because they trade like sheep, buying breakouts and chasing strength." — Roland Wolf (Wolf Trades homepage, confirmed)

Confirmation Requirements

  • Volume slows on the pullback (sellers exhausting)
  • Price stabilizes at or near support level
  • Bounce candle shows buyers stepping back in
  • Broader market not actively working against the setup
  • Short interest elevated (adds confidence, not required)

Aggressiveness vs. Patience

Roland is highly patient pre-entry but aggressively sized once the setup confirms. He does not scale into losers. He takes his full position near support with a defined stop.

Scaling In / Scaling Out

Adding Strategy

Only adds to a winner that is holding above entry. Does not average down into losing positions. If the support level fails, he cuts immediately.

Trimming Strategy

  • Scale 50%+ at first resistance / prior high
  • Trail stop on remaining "runners"
  • Full exit when momentum exhausts (volume falls, candles weaken)
  • Never hold runners through broad market weakness

Exit Logic

Roland sells into strength, not when the stock is pulling back again. This is the core discipline: buy the dip, sell the rip. He explicitly states this is the opposite of what most traders do.

The Two Golden Rules

Cut Losses Quickly

Tim Sykes Rule #1 — Roland repeats this constantly. Non-negotiable. If support fails, exit with no debate. Staying in a loser is the #1 account killer.

2

Position Size Appropriately

Cutting losses means nothing if your position is so large the small loss is still a catastrophe. Size matches the stop distance and account risk tolerance.

Stop Placement Logic
