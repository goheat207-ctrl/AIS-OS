Section 01 · Setup Decomposition — Structural Edge From Each Source Playbook Unified Small-Cap Trading OS

SETUP DECOMPOSITION

Each setup below is reverse-engineered from the source playbooks. Trader-specific personality, speed advantages, and institutional knowledge are removed. What remains is the transferable structural logic — the part that works because of market mechanics, not because of who is trading.

SETUP 1 · GAP & GO / MOMENTUM BREAKOUT
Source: Ross Cameron · Primary Intraday Setup

Core Thesis

A stock gaps up significantly pre-market on a real news catalyst. After the open, buyers continue to press higher rather than immediately filling the gap. The structural edge is the combination of short sellers trapped pre-market who must cover, FOMO buyers entering at the open, and low float amplifying every directional move. The highest-quality version has no selling pressure at all — it simply goes and never looks back.

Why it works mechanically: Shorts who faded the gap are underwater the moment price holds pre-market highs. Forced covering + new buyers = momentum expansion. Float scarcity amplifies this into a multi-point move.

Required Conditions (All Must Be Present)

  • Float under 20M shares preferred; hard ceiling at 50M for this setup
  • Price in $2–$20 range (above $20 reduces edge significantly per Cameron's data)
  • Relative volume 5x+ vs. 30-day average at time of setup
  • Intraday move 10%+ from prior close before entry
  • Verifiable news catalyst: earnings, FDA, partnership, contract, financing — NOT technical gap only
  • No confirmed dilution event: check authorized vs. outstanding shares, no ATM offering announced

Ideal Market Regime

Risk-On Momentum only. IWM above 20-day MA. VIX below 22. Small-cap breadth positive (more small-cap stocks above 50MA than below). At least one other small-cap running the same session confirms momentum regime is active. If the market is mixed or risk-off, this setup's follow-through rate drops sharply.

Trigger

First candle to make a new high after a clean consolidation above pre-market high, with volume expanding on the breakout candle. The consolidation must be 3–8 candles on a 1-minute chart with volume contracting. The breakout candle must be green and close near its high.

Invalidation

  • Gap fades immediately at the open — first candle is a large red engulfing
  • Volume is thin on the breakout candle (below average of the consolidation)
  • Large seller visible on Level 2 sitting at resistance
  • Broad market reverses sharply at open (SPY or IWM selling hard)
  • ATM offering or dilution news hits during pre-market
Failure Pattern
Gap opens strong, consolidates, breaks out with one candle, then immediately stalls. Volume dries up on the second candle. Price slowly drifts back into consolidation. This is a fakeout — exit immediately below the consolidation low, not after it "might recover."
Best Holding Profile
Intraday only. Hold through first 1–2 continuation legs. Partial profit at 1.5R–2R. Trail with 1-minute structure after first partial. Hard exit before 11:30 AM unless still actively expanding. Never hold overnight unless it also qualifies as Setup 5 (VCP).
Small-Cap Specific Risk
Halt risk is real. If stock halts up on continuation, assess liquidity on resume — wide spreads after halt = reduce size or exit. ATM offering can print at any moment on a gap run. Crowded retail attention means everyone sees the same setup — fake breakouts are common when too many traders are watching.
Source Attribution
Ross Cameron — 5-Pillar Framework, Gap and Go, breakout-or-bailout rule, Level 2 confirmation, RVOL requirement. Jesse Livermore — Pivotal point concept: the pre-market high is the exact pivot that must be broken to confirm the move.
SETUP 2 · BULL FLAG / INTRADAY CONTINUATION
Source: Ross Cameron + Ignition System · Secondary Intraday Setup

Core Thesis

After a strong initial directional move (the flagpole), price consolidates on declining volume before breaking to new highs. The structural edge is that impatient holders sell during the consolidation, reducing supply. When selling dries up and volume re-expands on the breakout, the move resumes because the only sellers have already exited. The Ignition System identifies the flat-top breakout variant as especially explosive: a large seller holds price at resistance until buyers overwhelm them, then that seller's removal causes a rapid squeeze of anyone with a stop just above.

Why the flat-top variant is most powerful: When a visible seller has been absorbing bids at a specific price for 5–15 candles, every short seller who entered near that level is relying on that seller. Once they disappear, all those stops trigger simultaneously.

Required Conditions

  • Strong flagpole: 3–8 candles of aggressive buying, high volume
  • Flag consolidation: 3–10 candles, volume declining each candle
  • Price holds above VWAP during consolidation
  • Price holds above prior day's close during consolidation
  • No new lows during the flag — higher lows required
  • Consolidation depth: no more than 38% of the flagpole move retraced
  • Same catalyst and float conditions as Setup 1

Trigger

First candle to make a new high after the flag (breaking above the highs of the consolidation candles), with volume surging on the breakout candle. Do not enter mid-flag or on the first red candle of the consolidation — wait for the full pattern to complete.

Invalidation
Flag candles show heavy red volume (distribution, not consolidation). Price breaks below the flag low. VWAP breaks during the flag. Second consolidation forms without a new high — momentum exhausting. Large seller appears on Level 2 at resistance.
Failure Pattern
Breakout triggers, but volume spike is one candle only. Second candle is weak. Price stalls below the prior high of day. This is a false breakout — classic retail trap. Exit on the second candle if it is red and closes in the lower half of its range.
Best Holding Profile
Intraday. Target measured move (flagpole length added to breakout point). First partial at 1.5R. Trail using 1-minute structure lows after second partial. If it becomes a parabolic extension (3× the flagpole), take the third partial and use a wider structure trail.
Small-Cap Specific Risk
Late-day flags (after 11:30 AM) on 1-minute charts have significantly lower follow-through. Switch to 5-minute chart after 11:30 AM — same pattern logic, different timeframe. Avoid flags that form on less than 50% of the opening volume rate.
SETUP 3 · STRUCTURED DIP BUY / VWAP RECLAIM
Source: Roland Wolf · Tertiary Intraday + Swing Setup

Core Thesis

After a catalyst-driven run, the stock pulls back to a defined support level. Short sellers who were correct about the pullback begin to feel comfortable. That comfort is the trap. Roland Wolf's edge is entering the pullback before the breakout attempt — so when the squeeze begins, the position is already in profit. The "Dip & Rip" variant is the purest version: a gap-up stock flushes hard at the open, shaking out weak longs, then rips back through pre-market highs as trapped shorts cover. The structural edge is identical to a short squeeze — forced buying from people who bet on the fade.

Why this differs from Setup 1: Setup 1 buys after the breakout is confirmed. Setup 3 buys at the support level that precedes the breakout. Different risk/reward profile — wider initial stop, earlier entry, better average price.

Required Conditions

  • Stock has a real catalyst (not a "chart looks good" argument)
  • Initial move already happened — this is a pullback entry, not a first move
  • Support level is predefined and specific: prior-day high, VWAP, whole number ($5, $10, $15), or pre-market low
  • Volume declining as price approaches support (selling is exhausting)
  • Low float or confirmed short interest — squeeze fuel must exist
  • No dilution event during the pullback (check news on the red candles)
  • Minimum 2:1 risk-reward exists between support and next resistance

Trigger

Volume dries up at support level AND price produces a green candle with tail rejection off the support. Do not enter while selling is still active. Wait for the first sign of buying absorption — a candle that wicks down and closes in the upper half of its range on the support level.

Invalidation
Support level breaks with volume — not a dip, it's a breakdown. Catalyst proven false or walked back. No buyers show up at support (volume stays low with no green candle formation). Broad market selloff overrides individual catalyst.
Failure Pattern
Support holds once, provides a small bounce, then fails on the second test. This is the "second test failure" — if price returns to support within 3–5 candles and breaks it, the structural thesis is invalidated. Exit immediately.
Best Holding Profile
Intraday through to swing depending on catalyst strength. First target: reclaim of VWAP or prior consolidation zone. Second target: prior high of day. If catalyst is multi-day, this can become a swing hold — but requires qualifying as Setup 5 conditions on the daily chart.
Small-Cap Specific Risk
Dilution risk is highest during pullbacks — companies have incentive to sell shares when price is elevated. Averaging down trap: if support breaks, do NOT add. The stock may be beginning a real reversal, not a deeper dip. One entry at support, not a scaling down strategy.
SETUP 4 · FIRST GREEN DAY (FGD) / FEAR EXHAUSTION REVERSAL
Source: The Ignition System · Roland Wolf LOD Reversal · Catalyst Swing Setup

Core Thesis

A stock has fallen for 2–5 consecutive days on fear, capitulation, or sector rotation. Short sellers are stacked at elevated prices and feeling confident. Then a credible catalyst arrives. The first day that price closes above the prior day's close is the First Green Day — the exact moment short sellers' P&L flips from green to red. Their collective panic covering, combined with momentum traders re-entering, creates a violent upside move. The Ignition System identifies this as the structural mirror of the First Red Day shorting setup — the same mechanics, opposite direction. The previous day's close is the single most important level.

Why the prior close matters more than any other level: Short sellers track their positions versus prior close in real time. A stock that closes green vs. yesterday creates immediate margin pressure on anyone short from the previous session. This is forced buying, not optional buying.

Required Conditions (All Must Be Present)

  • Minimum 2–3 consecutive red daily candles preceding the setup
  • 30–50%+ decline from the recent swing high into the washout
  • Elevated short interest: above 10% of float preferred, above 15% ideal
  • Declining candle size into the lows (selling exhausting, not accelerating)
  • A verifiable, credible catalyst on the reversal morning — NOT just "oversold"
  • Float preference: under 20M; ceiling at 50M
  • No fundamental deterioration: check for going-concern warnings, toxic financing, bankruptcy risk
  • Dilution check: authorized vs. outstanding ratio must not suggest imminent offering

Trigger (Choose One Based on Experience)

Standard: Price breaks above the prior day's close on expanding volume — enter on confirmation. Aggressive: Enter at predefined support level during the washout low with stop below LOD — better R/R, lower win rate. Conservative: Wait for breakout above prior close, then buy the first higher-low pullback — highest win rate, worst entry price.

Ideal Market Regime
Squeeze-Heavy or Risk-On Momentum. Short sellers must be actively present — check short interest before entry. Works poorly in risk-off or low-liquidity environments where there is no squeeze fuel. Requires at least moderate small-cap breadth.
Invalidation
Price reclaims prior close intraday but fails to hold it on the close — failed FGD. Fundamental deterioration discovered (toxic financing, reverse split risk, going concern). No short interest to squeeze — the reversal has no fuel. Broad market in confirmed downtrend overrides the individual catalyst.
Failure Pattern
Stock breaks above prior close with volume, looks like a confirmed FGD, then reverses sharply on news that validates the short sellers (e.g., dilutive offering, negative trial result follow-up, SEC investigation). This is the "false FGD" — the catalyst was real but the fundamental problem overrides it. Defend with: cap exposure at 1% even for A+ FGD setups.
Small-Cap Specific Risk
FGD setups are the highest dilution risk window in this system. Companies in multi-day washouts have institutional pressure to raise capital. Check authorized shares, recent S-3 filings, and ATM agreement history before every FGD entry. Maximum exposure: 1% regardless of grade when dilution risk is present.
SETUP 5 · VCP / VOLATILITY CONTRACTION (SWING)
Source: Minervini VCP + Livermore Normal Reaction · Swing Hold Setup

Core Thesis

A stock in a Stage 2 uptrend (all moving averages stacked, price near 52-week highs) forms a base where each successive pullback is smaller than the last and volume dries up progressively. This is institutional accumulation leaving a footprint. Each tighter contraction proves fewer sellers remain. At the final pivot point — an extremely tight range with near-zero volume — even modest new demand sends price violently higher. Livermore's "Normal Reaction Continuation" is the same logic: a healthy pullback on light volume after a breakout is the add-to-winner opportunity. The pivot is Livermore's pivotal point applied to a multi-week structure.

Key distinction from Setups 1–4: This setup requires Stage 2 trend alignment on the daily chart. It is the only setup in this system that uses fundamental pre-filters. It is the swing hold mechanism — it is not used intraday.

Required Conditions

  • Stage 2 Check (Minervini 8-Point): Price above 150-day and 200-day MA; 150-day above 200-day; 200-day trending up 1+ month; 50-day above both; price above 50-day; price 30%+ above 52-week low; price within 25% of 52-week high
  • 2–6 successive contractions, each smaller than the last in both price range and volume
  • No lower lows through the base — only higher lows
  • Volume declining on each successive contraction
  • Final pivot: price range tightens to less than 5–8% over 3–10 days
  • For small-cap application: catalyst history required (not just technical setup)

Trigger

Price breaks 1 cent above the pivot resistance on a volume surge (50%+ above 30-day average). Enter at the breakout, not before. Do not anticipate — the pivot must be broken to confirm supply exhaustion.

Invalidation
Breakout on light volume (below 30-day average) — institutional buyers are not participating. Price returns below the pivot within 3 days — supply not fully exhausted. Stage 2 criteria broken (price closes below 50-day MA with volume). Earnings deceleration reported.
Failure Pattern
Pivot breakout occurs, price moves 3–5% higher, then reverses sharply on no news. This is distribution — institutions used the breakout to sell into buyers. Defend with: hard stop at 7–8% below entry and exit on first close below the 10-day MA.
Best Holding Profile
Multi-day to multi-week. First partial at 2R. Trail with 10-day MA close (exit if daily close below 10-day MA). Full exit on abnormal reversal volume (2× average on a large red day). Livermore principle: sit through normal reactions; only exit on abnormal behavior.
Small-Cap Specific Risk
Small-cap VCPs are noisier than Minervini's mid/large-cap applications. Require stricter float quality — avoid stocks with pending share offerings even in Stage 2. Reduce stop to 5–6% (tighter than Minervini's 7–8%) on stocks under $10 due to gap risk overnight.

Section 03 · Unified Trading Architecture — The Daily Operating System Unified Small-Cap Trading OS

UNIFIED TRADING ARCHITECTURE

Daily Decision Flow — Step by Step

This is the sequence you run every morning before any trade is taken. Do not skip steps. Each step is a gate — if a gate fails, you do not proceed to the next one. This prevents trading in bad conditions.

1
Pre-Market Regime Check (4:00–8:30 AM)

Check VIX. Check IWM position relative to 20-day MA and 50-day MA. Count how many small-cap stocks are up 10%+ pre-market with real news. If VIX is above 28 AND IWM is below its 50MA: declare Risk-Off. No Setup 1 or 2 today. If you see zero legitimate small-cap runners pre-market: stand down on Setups 1–4. Only Setup 5 candidates remain active.

2
Scanner + Watchlist Build (6:00–9:00 AM)

Run pre-market scanner: float under 50M, RVOL 3x+, pre-market change +5% to +40%, price $2–$20. Every alert gets a news check immediately. If no verifiable catalyst: delete from watchlist. Rank remaining by setup type. Mark prior-day highs, pre-market highs, VWAP, and key whole numbers on every name. This is your support/resistance map for the session.

3
Dilution Check (Before Open)

For every stock on your watchlist: check authorized shares vs. outstanding. Check for recent S-3 or S-1 filings. Check for existing ATM agreement. Check for warrants near current price. If dilution risk is present: reduce maximum position size to 0.5R and place a warning flag. If fundamental deterioration is present (going concern, toxic financing): remove entirely.

4
Score Each Candidate (Section 5 Scoring Model)

Apply the 10-criterion scoring model to each watchlist name. Minimum score to trade: 65 for intraday setups (1–4), 75 for swing setups (5). Names scoring below threshold go on "watch but no trade" list. Do not trade a setup just because you've been watching it all morning and "feel" ready — the score must qualify it.

5
Open Observation (9:30–9:50 AM)

Do NOT enter any trade in the first 5 minutes on small caps unless you are pre-loaded from the pre-market. Observe how your watchlist names open: are they gapping and going, or fading? Are the strongest pre-market names holding their gains or selling off? This tells you whether the day's momentum regime is real. Two or more names following through = active momentum regime. Two or more names fading = rotation day, lean toward Setup 3 and 4.

6
First Trade Window (9:50 AM–11:30 AM)

This is the primary intraday execution window. Setups 1, 2, 3, and 4 are all active. Take only the highest-scoring setup first. Apply full entry rules (Section 6). Maximum 2 simultaneous open positions during this window. After 11:30 AM: Setups 1 and 2 are disabled. Only Setup 3 (if structure holds), Setup 4 (if FGD has not yet triggered), and Setup 5 (daily management) remain.

7
Mid-Day Assessment (11:30 AM–2:00 PM)

Switch all active intraday charts from 1-minute to 5-minute. Re-assess any open positions: are they still above key levels, or drifting? Swing setup candidates: check daily chart for VCP progress. No new intraday entries unless a Setup 4 FGD trigger occurs on a catalyst that just broke. Keep a hard mental stop on total day loss.

8
End of Day Review (After Close)

Record every trade: setup type, score, entry, stop, exit, R multiple result, and one-line reason for entry. Check if daily loss limit was hit. If yes: log it and check whether loss was due to bad execution (rules broken) or valid setup that failed (outcome variance). Check current portfolio drawdown level vs. risk throttle thresholds.

Watchlist Ranking Logic

Each morning, rank your watchlist names using this priority sequence:

  1. Score 75+: Primary focus. Allocate first attention and capital.
  2. Score 65–74: Secondary watch. Trade only if Score 75+ names fail to set up.
  3. Score below 65: Watch only. No trade regardless of how strong the move looks.
  4. Dilution flag present: Separate watchlist. Hard cap 0.5R. No exceptions.
  5. FGD candidates: Separate list with catalyst verification check every 15 minutes during washout sequence.
Section 04 · Regime Engine — How to Read Market Conditions and Route Your Setup Priority Unified Small-Cap Trading OS

REGIME ENGINE

The regime engine is the brain of the system. It tells you which setups are live, which are disabled, how large to trade, and how long to hold. A setup that has a 70% win rate in the right regime can have a 35% win rate in the wrong one. Regime classification happens pre-market and is re-assessed at 11:30 AM intraday.

The two regime inputs: (1) VIX level — measures market-wide fear/volatility. (2) IWM position relative to 20-day and 50-day MA — measures small-cap market health specifically. These two inputs classify every trading day into one of six regimes before you see a single stock.
REGIME 1 · RISK-ON MOMENTUM FULL ACTIVATION

Classification Criteria

  • VIX below 20
  • IWM above 20-day MA and 50-day MA (both)
  • Small-cap breadth: 60%+ of small-cap universe above 50-day MA
  • 2+ legitimate small-cap runners visible pre-market with real catalysts
  • Setup follow-through in recent 5 sessions: at least 60% of setups reaching 1.5R+

Active Setups (Priority Order)

Setup 1 → Setup 2 → Setup 3 → Setup 4 → Setup 5. All setups active. Setups 1 and 2 are the primary focus.

Operational Adjustments

  • Size: Full base size (1.0–1.25% per trade)
  • Hold time: Intraday through multi-day — let momentum run
  • Confirmation threshold: Standard — no extra confirmation required beyond setup rules
  • Profit-taking: First partial at 2R (not 1.5R). Let winners run further.
  • Capital split: 70% intraday / 30% swing
Regime Behavior: Breakouts follow through. Dips are bought. Catalysts hold. This is when Ross Cameron's aggressive scaling and Livermore's "sit tight" principle both apply simultaneously.
REGIME 2 · SQUEEZE-HEAVY SELECTIVE ACTIVATION

Classification Criteria

  • VIX 18–26 (elevated but not extreme)
  • IWM mixed — near 20-day MA, may be above or below
  • Multiple FGD-type setups appearing simultaneously (3+ in one week)
  • Short interest in small caps elevated (sector-wide or market-wide)
  • Recent failed breakouts + successful squeeze reversals confirm this regime

Active Setups

Setup 4 (FGD) primary. Setup 3 (Dip Buy) secondary. Setup 5 (VCP) for swing. Setups 1 and 2 active but reduced size — fake breakouts are common in this regime.

Operational Adjustments

  • Size: 75% of base on Setups 1–2. Full base on Setup 4.
  • Hold time: FGD trades: allow multi-day if catalyst supports. Intraday momentum: tighter.
  • Confirmation threshold: Require short interest confirmation before FGD entry (check in real time)
  • Profit-taking: First partial at 1.5R. Tighter trail after second partial.
  • Capital split: 50% intraday / 50% swing
REGIME 3 · MIXED / ROTATIONAL REDUCED ACTIVATION

Classification Criteria

  • VIX 20–26
  • IWM oscillating around 20-day MA — no clear trend
  • Small-cap breadth mixed: 40–60% above 50-day MA
  • Sector rotation visible — momentum shifts between sectors within single sessions
  • Breakouts from the last 5 sessions showing less than 50% follow-through to 1.5R

Active Setups

Setup 5 (VCP swing) primary. Setup 3 (Dip Buy) secondary. Setup 4 (FGD) if short interest extremely high. Setups 1 and 2 reduced to 50% size only. Do not chase breakouts in rotational regime.

Operational Adjustments

  • Size: 50% of base on all intraday setups. Full base on Setup 5 only.
  • Hold time: Intraday only for Setups 1–4. No overnight holds on intraday momentum.
  • Confirmation threshold: Higher bar — require two confirming factors vs. standard one
  • Profit-taking: First partial at 1.25R. Do not hold for 2R in rotational regime.
  • Capital split: 40% intraday / 60% swing
Regime Warning: This is where over-engineering kills you. You will see setups everywhere. Most will fail. The discipline is to trade fewer, smaller, and demand more confirmation.
REGIME 4 · RISK-OFF / FAILURE-PRONE MINIMAL ACTIVATION

Classification Criteria

  • VIX above 28
  • IWM below 50-day MA
  • Small-cap breadth: under 40% above 50-day MA
  • Recent breakouts failing at rates above 70%
  • Multiple gap-up stocks reversing hard the same day

Active Setups

Setup 5 (VCP) only — and only highest-quality Stage 2 names. All intraday momentum setups (1, 2, 3, 4) are disabled until regime improves.

Operational Adjustments

  • Size: 50% of base on Setup 5 only
  • Hold time: Swing holds only — no intraday
  • Capital split: 0% intraday / 50% swing / 50% cash
  • Priority: Preserve capital. Use this regime to study setups, refine your scoring model, and wait for conditions to improve.
Cameron's Trader Rehab Rule: In risk-off, reduce size, reduce frequency, return to best core setups only. Do not strategy-hop. Stand down is a position.
REGIME 5 · DILUTION-HEAVY SYSTEM-WIDE OVERRIDE

Trigger

3+ dilution events (ATM offerings, convertible notes, secondary offerings) in small-cap stocks within a 5-day window. This signals that issuers are raising capital aggressively — and any stock you hold is at risk.

Rules

  • All new long entries reduced to 0.5R maximum regardless of setup quality
  • All swing holds re-examined for dilution risk — reduce or exit any with dilution exposure

Section 06 · Execution Rules — Exact Rules for Every Phase of a Trade Unified Small-Cap Trading OS

EXECUTION RULES

Starter Size Rules

The first entry is never full size. This protects against entering a setup that immediately fails before you have time to reassess. A partial entry gives you information — if it moves immediately, you add. If it stalls, you exit with a fraction of the intended loss.

Initial Position Sizing

  • Score 85–100: Enter with 60% of planned position at the trigger
  • Score 75–84: Enter with 50% of planned position at the trigger
  • Score 65–74: Enter with 40% of planned position at the trigger
  • Dilution flag present: Enter with 30% regardless of score
  • First trade after a losing streak (3+ losses): Enter with 30% of planned position only
Livermore's probe principle: The first entry is a probe. You are buying information, not just shares. If the market confirms your thesis immediately, you already have a position. If it contradicts your thesis immediately, your loss is small.

Add Criteria — When to Complete the Position

Adding to a position is only allowed when the trade is already working. You are adding into strength, not adding into a loss to average down. Roland Wolf's system, Minervini's system, and Livermore's pyramiding approach all share this identical rule.

Add Rules (All Must Be Met)

  • The initial entry is at breakeven or profitable — not underwater
  • Price has made a new high since entry OR broken the key level that was the setup trigger
  • Volume is expanding, not contracting, on the add candle
  • The setup trigger remains valid — no invalidation has occurred
  • Adding does not cause total position to exceed the planned full-size risk
  • For Setup 5 (VCP): add only after a "normal reaction" pullback on light volume (Livermore rule)

Scaling Out — The Exit Ladder

Scaling out is not optional — it is the mechanism that locks in realized profit while leaving exposure to further upside. The Ignition System's "sell into thirds" and Roland Wolf's "partial scaling discipline" are unified here into one rule set.

Exit Ladder for Intraday Setups (1–4)

  1. First partial (1/3 of position): At 1.5R from entry. Locks in first confirmed gain. Move stop to breakeven on remaining 2/3.
  2. Second partial (1/3 of position): At 2R–2.5R. Or at a significant resistance level (prior high, round number) if that is below 2R.
  3. Final exit (remaining 1/3): On first candle that closes red after a strong move, OR at hard time-stop (11:30 AM for Setup 1/2), OR on Level 2 showing large seller appearing.
Adjustment for strong momentum: In Risk-On regime, if the stock accelerates past 3R without a reversal signal, delay the second partial and trail the stop instead. Livermore: "The big money is made in the big moves."

Exit Ladder for Swing Setups (Setup 5)

  1. First partial (1/3): At 2R from pivot entry. Locks in profit on the first leg.
  2. Second partial (1/3): At 4R–5R or at a major resistance level. Move stop to 1R above entry on remaining shares.
  3. Final exit: Daily close below 10-day MA with volume. OR abnormal reversal: single day down 2×+ average volume on a large red candle. OR fundamental change (dilution, earnings deceleration).

Chase Prevention Rules

These rules have one job: prevent you from entering a setup after the optimal entry window has passed. Every one of these rules comes from painful documented mistakes in the source playbooks.

  • ✗Do not enter Setup 1 if the stock has already moved more than 40% from its prior close before your entry
  • ✗Do not enter a bull flag that has been consolidating for more than 45 minutes on the 1-minute chart — the momentum is stale
  • ✗Do not enter any intraday setup after 11:30 AM on a 1-minute chart — switch to 5-minute or exit the watchlist
  • ✗Do not enter a Setup 4 FGD if the prior day's close has not been clearly broken AND held for at least 2 candles on the 5-minute chart
  • ✗Do not add to a losing position under any circumstances — averaging down is not in this system
  • ✗Do not enter a VCP pivot breakout on low volume (below 30-day average on the breakout day)

Halt-Risk Adjustment

Before Any Entry

Check if the stock is near a price level that typically triggers trading halts (circuit breakers at +10%, +20%, +50% intraday moves). If the stock is approaching a halt level and you are not already in the trade: wait for the resume and assess spread before entering. Do not chase into a halt.

During a Halt

  • Do not assume direction. The halt could be up or down news.
  • On resume: check bid-ask spread immediately. If spread is above 2% of price, do not add — exit cleanly instead.
  • If you are already in the trade and the halt resumes against you: exit at market rather than waiting for your stop. Halts eliminate your stop placement entirely.
Section 07 · Risk Operating System — Hard Rules That Cannot Be Overridden Unified Small-Cap Trading OS

RISK OPERATING SYSTEM

The Risk OS is not a suggestion layer. It is a hard constraint system. Every rule in this section overrides every other consideration — including a "perfect" setup, a hot catalyst, or a strong conviction. The source for these rules: Cameron's daily max loss and trader rehab, Roland Wolf's defined risk per trade, Ignition System's position sizing by grade, and Livermore's explicit statements about the necessity of cash preservation above all else.

The Five Hard Limits

1

Risk Per Trade: 0.75% to 1.25% of total account

Calculated as (entry price − stop price) × shares ÷ account value. Never exceed 1.25% on any single trade regardless of score. In reduced-size regimes, never exceed 0.5%. Score determines where in the 0.75–1.25 range you land, not higher.

2

Maximum Portfolio Heat: 4% to 6%

The sum of all open trade risks at any moment cannot exceed 6%. In mixed/rotational regime: cap at 4%. In risk-off: cap at 2%. Calculate this before every new entry — if adding the new trade would exceed the heat limit, you do not enter until an existing position is reduced.

3

Daily Maximum Loss: 2R (2× your standard single-trade risk)

If you are risking 1% per trade, your daily max loss is 2% of account. Hit this limit and the trading day is over — not paused, not "I'll try one more." Cameron: once daily max is hit, stop for the day. No exceptions. Revenge trading begins exactly at this threshold.

4

Weekly Drawdown Trigger: -4% of account

If the account drops 4% in a single week, reduce risk per trade to 0.5% for the following week. Do not return to full size until you have a profitable week at reduced size. This is the "rehab" protocol — not punishment, just calibration.

5

Portfolio Drawdown Hard Stop: -10% to -12%

Full stand-down. All new trades suspended. No exceptions. Review every loss from the drawdown period. Identify: was the system broken (rules not followed) or were valid setups failing due to regime mismatch? Only re-enter when regime improves AND re-risk criteria are fully met.

Drawdown Throttling Protocol

This is a staged response to increasing drawdown. The goal is to slow down during the periods when the system or the market is not working, while staying active enough to recover. Livermore described this principle in terms of staying solvent to capitalize on the big opportunities.

A losing streak is a separate trigger from a drawdown, because you can lose 3 trades in a row while still being within your drawdown limit if they were small losses.

  • 5 consecutive losses: Stop for that trading day. No more entries. Review regime and confirm you're not in a deteriorating market.
  • 7 consecutive losses: Enter reduced-size protocol as if at -6% drawdown, regardless of actual P&L. Something is wrong with execution or regime alignment.

  • ✓No open positions: Return to full size only at the start of a new trading week, not mid-week during recovery
Section 08 · Small-Cap Failure Prevention — The 10 Ways This System Can Destroy You Unified Small-Cap Trading OS

SMALL-CAP FAILURE PREVENTION

Every rule in this section addresses a specific, documented failure mode in small-cap trading. These are not theoretical risks — each one has appeared in the source playbooks as a reason for real losses. Know them before they cost you.

1
Calling Every Green Candle a First Green Day

The Ignition System explicitly identifies this as the most common FGD error. A single green candle in the middle of a downtrend is noise — not a setup. The FGD requires a complete multi-day washout sequence (minimum 2–3 consecutive red days) and a verifiable catalyst.

Defense Rule: Before labeling any setup a FGD, count the red days: must be 2 or more. Check for a catalyst: must be verifiable. If either fails, it is not a FGD — do not trade it as one.
2
Ignoring Float and Short Interest (Entering Without the Squeeze Fuel)

High-float stocks on weak catalysts produce the same chart patterns as low-float squeeze setups — but without the squeeze mechanics. Without trapped shorts, there is no forced covering. Without forced covering, there is no secondary fuel. The move will be weak.

Defense Rule: Float check is mandatory before every entry. No float data = no trade. Float above 50M on Setups 1–4 = reduce score by 10 points minimum.
3
Dilution Ambush — Offering Announced During Your Trade

A company running 30% on a catalyst will often use that elevated price to issue shares via an ATM offering or secondary. This is not coincidence — it is by design. The company's investment bankers are watching the price too. The offering drops the stock 20–40% instantly and your long position is trapped.

Defense Rule: Before every trade, check: (1) authorized vs. outstanding ratio, (2) existing ATM agreement, (3) recent S-3 filing activity. If any red flag is present, hard cap at 0.5R and widen your awareness of sudden news. Set a price alert for 8-K filings on every open position.
4
Trading the Wrong Regime (Running Risk-On Setups in Risk-Off)

Setup 1 (Gap & Go) can look identical in both Risk-On and Risk-Off regimes on a stock chart. The difference is that in Risk-Off, the follow-through rate collapses. You will take a series of technically valid setups that all fail. This is regime mismatch, not execution failure — but it destroys accounts the same way.

Defense Rule: Regime check is the first step every morning (Step 1 of the Daily Decision Flow). If regime is Risk-Off or Mixed, disable Setup 1 and 2 before looking at any individual stock setups.
5
Averaging Down Into a Falling Knife

The Ignition System identifies this as "the deadliest mistake in small-cap longs." A stock falling on no new catalyst is not "cheaper" — it may be going to zero. The only acceptable add to a small-cap position is into strength after the thesis is confirmed, never into a weakening position to reduce the average cost.

Defense Rule: If the stock breaks the defined stop: exit in full. No averaging down, no "giving it room," no "waiting for a bounce to get out." One entry, one stop, one exit. This rule has zero exceptions.
6
Holding an Intraday Setup Overnight as a Swing Trade

A trade that qualifies as Setup 1 (Gap & Go intraday) is not automatically a Setup 5 (VCP swing) just because it's still moving at 3 PM. Overnight gap risk in small caps is extreme. A company can issue shares, receive negative news, or get halted between sessions. Holding a day trade overnight requires the stock to ALSO qualify on the daily chart as a swing setup.

Defense Rule: Before holding any intraday position overnight, run the full Setup 5 checklist on the daily chart. If it fails the Stage 2 or VCP criteria, exit before close regardless of P&L.
7
Crowded Retail Attention — The "Everyone Sees It" Trap

When a small-cap setup is visible on every retail trading forum simultaneously, the edge degrades. Every retail trader buying the bull flag simultaneously means the flag break is bought by everyone at once — and sold into by the institutional player who set up the pattern. The Ignition System warns that the cleanest-looking setups in crowded names often fail fastest.

Defense Rule: If a stock has been trending on social media for 6+ hours before you see the setup, reduce score by 5 points. The best setups are ones you found first — not ones that are already viral. Reduce size by 25% on any stock that was trending on retail platforms overnight.
8
Fake Breakout Defense — Entering Before Confirmation

Ross Cameron's breakout-or-bailout rule and Roland Wolf's "wait for the pattern to complete" are both responses to the same failure: entering a setup before the trigger is confirmed. Anticipating the trigger is a personality-driven behavior (you "feel" it's about to break). The system requires the actual trigger to fire.

Defense Rule: Define the exact trigger price before entering. If you are entering before that price is hit: you are anticipating, not confirming. Anticipatory entries are not permitted by this system. Wait for the candle to close past the trigger, then enter.
9
Warrant and Conversion Overhang — Hidden Supply

Warrants outstanding at prices near or below the current stock price represent future selling pressure that is invisible on the chart. When the stock rallies to or above warrant strike prices, warrant holders can convert and sell — creating a price ceiling that looks like "resistance" but is actually structured dilution.

Defense Rule: For any stock held more than intraday, check for outstanding warrants in the most recent 10-K or S-1. If warrant strike prices are within 20% of current price, flag as dilution risk and cap at 0.5R.
10
Strategy Drift Under Pressure — Running the Wrong Setup in the Wrong Regime

After a losing streak, traders often start using setups they weren't planning to use, in regimes those setups don't fit, at sizes they shouldn't be trading. This is not strategic adaptation — it is desperation. Cameron's trader rehab protocol, Minervini's focus on only Stage 2, and Livermore's explicit rule about staying out when conditions are unclear all address this: when your setup isn't working, the answer is fewer trades at smaller size — not different strategies at larger size.

Defense Rule: Maintain a written list of the currently active regime and the approved setups for that regime. Before any trade, verify you are trading an approved setup for the current regime. If you are considering a setup not on the approved list: stop, wait until the regime permits it or pass.

SIMPLIFIED IMMEDIATE SYSTEM

The Simplified Morning Routine

  1. Check VIX. Above 28? Only Setup 5 today. Above 35? Stand down entirely.
  2. Check IWM. Below 50-day MA? Reduce all sizes by 50%.
  3. Find your stock. Float under 20M. Real news. Pre-market up 10%+. RVOL 5x+.
  4. Check dilution. Does the company have an ATM offering or authorized shares 5x+ outstanding? If yes: pass or max 0.5R.
  5. Mark your levels. Pre-market high. Prior day close. VWAP. Two whole numbers above and below.
  6. Score it quickly. Use a simplified version: Catalyst (strong/medium/weak). Float (good/ok/bad). RVOL (high/medium/low). 3 "goods" = trade. Anything less = reduce size or pass.

The Simplified Entry

Wait for the stock to break above the pre-market high OR the prior day's close (whichever is relevant to your setup). Wait for a candle to CLOSE above that level on the 1-minute chart — not just touch it. Enter with 50% of your planned size at that candle close. Set your stop below the low of that candle. Your plan is now locked in.

The Simplified Exit

First: When profit equals your stop distance (1R), sell half. Move your stop to breakeven. You cannot lose money on this trade anymore.

Second: When profit equals 2× your stop distance (2R), sell another quarter. Trail the last quarter with the previous candle's low as your stop.

Hard rule: If the trade doesn't show profit within 5 candles: exit in full. The best setups work immediately.

Simplified Risk Rules

  • Risk no more than 1% of your account on any single trade
  • Maximum two trades open at the same time
  • If you lose 2%+ in one day: stop trading for the day
  • If you lose 3 trades in a row: stop and review before the next trade
  • After 11:30 AM: no new intraday entries unless a fresh catalyst just broke
  • Never average down into a losing position
  • Never hold a day trade overnight without deliberate review
